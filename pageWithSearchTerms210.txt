<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Requirements engineering</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Requirements engineering</b> (RE)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> refers to the process of defining, documenting and maintaining <a href="/wiki/Requirement" title="Requirement">requirements</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> to the sub-fields of <a href="/wiki/Systems_engineering" title="Systems engineering">systems engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a> concerned with this process.</p>
<p>The first use of the term <i>requirements engineering</i> was probably in 1979 in a <a href="/wiki/TRW_Inc." title="TRW Inc.">TRW</a> technical report<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> but did not come into general use until the 1990s with the publication of an <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Computer_Society" title="IEEE Computer Society">IEEE Computer Society</a> tutorial<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the establishment of a conference series on requirements engineering that has evolved into the current <a href="/wiki/International_Requirements_Engineering_Conference" title="International Requirements Engineering Conference">International Requirements Engineering Conference</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Waterfall_model" title="Waterfall model">waterfall model</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> requirements engineering is presented as the first phase of the development process. Later software development methods, including the <a href="/wiki/Rational_Unified_Process" title="Rational Unified Process">Rational Unified Process</a> (RUP), <a href="/wiki/Extreme_programming" title="Extreme programming">extreme programming</a> (XP) and <a href="/wiki/Scrum_(software_development)" title="Scrum (software development)">Scrum</a> assume that requirements engineering continues through the lifetime of a system.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alan_M._Davis" title="Alan M. Davis">Alan M. Davis</a> maintains an extensive bibliography of requirements engineering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Requirement management which is a sub-function of Systems Engineering practices are also indexed in the INCOSE (International Council on Systems Engineering) manuals.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Requirements_engineering_activities">Requirements engineering activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Requirements_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Requirements engineering activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The activities involved in requirements engineering vary widely, depending on the type of system being developed and the specific practices of the organization(s) involved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> These may include:</p>
<ol>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Requirements_inception&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Requirements inception (page does not exist)">Requirements inception</a> or <a href="/wiki/Requirements_elicitation" title="Requirements elicitation">requirements elicitation</a> -</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Requirements_identification&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Requirements identification (page does not exist)">Requirements identification</a> - identifying new requirements</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Requirements_analysis" title="Requirements analysis">Requirements analysis</a> and negotiation - checking requirements and resolving stakeholder conflicts</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_requirements_specification" title="Software requirements specification">Requirements specification</a> (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Software_requirements_specification" title="Software requirements specification">software requirements specification</a>; SRS) - documenting the requirements in a requirements document</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Systems_modeling" title="Systems modeling">Systems modeling</a> - deriving models of the system, often using a notation such as the <a href="/wiki/Unified_Modeling_Language" title="Unified Modeling Language">Unified Modeling Language</a> (UML) or the <a href="/wiki/Lifecycle_Modeling_Language" title="Lifecycle Modeling Language">Lifecycle Modeling Language</a> (LML)</li>
<li>Requirements validation - checking that the documented requirements and models are consistent and meet stakeholder needs</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Requirements_management" title="Requirements management">Requirements management</a> - managing changes to the requirements as the system is developed and put into use</li>
</ol>
<p>These are sometimes presented as chronological stages although, in practice, there is considerable interleaving of these activities.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Problems">Problems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Requirements_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Problems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One limited study in Germany presented possible problems in implementing requirements engineering and asked respondents whether they agreed that they were actual problems. The results were not presented as being generalizable but suggested that the principal perceived problems were incomplete requirements, moving targets, and time boxing, with lesser problems being communications flaws, lack of traceability, terminological problems, and unclear responsibilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Requirements_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some recent research suggests that software requirements are often an <a href="/wiki/Illusion" title="Illusion">illusion</a> misrepresenting design decisions as requirements in situations where no real requirements are evident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
