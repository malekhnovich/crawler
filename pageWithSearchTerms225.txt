<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Offshore outsourcing</h1>

<p><b>Offshore outsourcing</b> is the practice of hiring an external organization to perform some business functions ("<a href="/wiki/Outsourcing" title="Outsourcing">Outsourcing</a>") in a country other than the one where the <a href="/wiki/Product_(business)" title="Product (business)">products</a> or <a href="/wiki/Service_(economics)" title="Service (economics)">services</a> are actually developed or manufactured ("<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Offshore" title="wikt:Offshore">Offshore</a>"). It can be contrasted with <a href="/wiki/Offshoring" title="Offshoring">offshoring</a>, in which a company moves itself entirely to another country, or where functions are performed in a foreign country by a foreign subsidiary. Opponents point out that the practice of sending work overseas by countries with higher <a href="/wiki/Wage" title="Wage">wages</a> reduces their own domestic employment and domestic investment. Many customer service jobs as well as jobs in the <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> sectors (<a href="/wiki/Data_processing" title="Data processing">data processing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_programming" title="Computer programming">computer programming</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Technical_support" title="Technical support">technical support</a>) in countries such as the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> - have been or are potentially affected.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offshore_outsourcing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are four basic types of offshore outsourcing:</p>
<ul>
<li>ITO or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Information_Technology_Outsourcing" title="Information Technology Outsourcing">Information Technology Outsourcing</a> is where outsourcing related to technology or internet such as computer programming.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Business_Process_Outsourcing" title="Business Process Outsourcing">Business Process Outsourcing</a> (BPO) involves contracting out of operational functions to a third party service provider.</li>
<li>Software R&amp;D — <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Offshore_programming" title="Offshore programming">offshore software development</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knowledge_Process_Outsourcing" title="Knowledge Process Outsourcing">Knowledge Process Outsourcing</a> (KPO) is a type of outsourcing which involves or requires a more advanced technical skill and a higher level of expertise.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria">Criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offshore_outsourcing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The general criteria for a job to be offshore-able are:</p>
<ul>
<li>There is a significant wage difference between the original and offshore countries;</li>
<li>The job can be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telework" title="Telework">telework</a>;</li>
<li>The work has a <span class="cleanup-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">high information content</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (November 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>;</li>
<li>The work can be transmitted over the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>;</li>
<li>The work is <span class="cleanup-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">easy to set up</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (November 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>;</li>
<li><span class="citation-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">The work is repeatable</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</li>
</ul>
<p>The driving factor behind the development of offshore outsourcing has been the need to cut costs while the enabling factor has been the global electronic internet network that allows digital data to be accessed and delivered instantly, from and to almost anywhere in the world.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_of_the_Internet">Impact of the Internet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offshore_outsourcing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Impact of the Internet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The widespread use and availability of the Internet has enabled individuals and small businesses to contract <a href="/wiki/Freelancer" title="Freelancer">freelancers</a> from all over the world to get projects done at a lower <a href="/wiki/Cost" title="Cost">cost</a> due to lower wages and property prices. <a href="/wiki/Crowdsourcing" title="Crowdsourcing">Crowdsourcing</a> systems such as <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Mechanical_Turk" title="Amazon Mechanical Turk">Mechanical Turk</a> and CrowdFlower have added the element of scalability, allowing businesses to outsource information tasks across the Internet to thousands of workers.</p>
<p>This trend runs in parallel with the tendency towards outsourcing in larger corporations, and may serve to strengthen <a href="/wiki/Small_business" title="Small business">small business</a>' capacity to compete with their larger competitors capable of setting up offshore locations, or of arriving at major contracts with offshore companies. <b>&lt;--- That sentence is so unclear.</b></p>
<p>On the other hand, according to Gartner, it also helped with accessibility to labor resources across the world, regardless of company size.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It gave rise to business models such as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Remote_In-Sourcing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Remote In-Sourcing (page does not exist)">Remote In-Sourcing</a> that allow companies to tap into resources found abroad, without losing control over security of product quality. The resulting ability to work remotely and online, will continue to have a huge impact on the overall development of the global economy, for small companies and for large corporations.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Source_of_conflict">Source of conflict</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Offshore_outsourcing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Source of conflict">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are different views on the impact of offshore outsourcing on the various societies affected, which reflects the attitude of <a href="/wiki/Protectionism" title="Protectionism">Protectionism</a> versus <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_Trade" title="Free Trade">Free Trade</a>. Some see it as a potential threat to the domestic job market in the developed world and ask for government protective measures (or at least closer scrutiny of existing trade practices), while others, including the countries who receive the work, see it as an opportunity. In fact, offshore outsourcing has led to Domestic factories and companies closing down leaving tens of thousands of U.S. workers jobless while underdeveloped countries such as Brazil, Turkey, etc. begin to flourish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Free-trade advocates suggest economies as a whole will obtain a net benefit from labor offshoring, but it is unclear if the displaced receive a net benefit.</p>
<p>Many companies offshore outsource because the wages are cheaper in other areas. This is beginning to create a conflict because many wages overseas are raising. For example, a study by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics found that Chinese wages were almost tripled in the seven years following 2002. The wage increases could change the set up of offshore outsourcing in years to come.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>One issue offshoring of technical services has brought more attention to is the value of education as an alleged solution to trade-related displacements. Education may no longer be a <a href="/wiki/Comparative_advantage" title="Comparative advantage">comparative advantage</a> of high-wage nations because the cost of education may be lower in the nations involved in the controversy. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.latimes.com/news/education/la-na-outsource6mar06,1,3461071.story?page=1&amp;coll=la-news-learning&amp;ctrack=1&amp;cset=true" rel="nofollow">[1]</a> While it is true that education is usually considered helpful to competitiveness in general, an "education <a href="/wiki/Arms_race" title="Arms race">arms race</a>" with low-wage nations may not pay off.</p>
