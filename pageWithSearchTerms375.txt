<p><b>Ontology engineering</b> in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Information_science" title="Information science">information science</a> is a field which studies the methods and methodologies for building <a href="/wiki/Ontology_(information_science)" title="Ontology (information science)">ontologies</a>: formal representations of a set of concepts within a domain and the relationships between those concepts. A large-scale representation of abstract concepts such as actions, time, physical objects and beliefs would be an example of ontological engineering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Ontology engineering is one of the areas of <a href="/wiki/Applied_ontology" title="Applied ontology">applied ontology</a>, and can be seen as an application of <a href="/wiki/Ontology" title="Ontology">philosophical ontology</a>. Core ideas and objectives of ontology engineering are also central in <a href="/wiki/Conceptual_model_(computer_science)" title="Conceptual model (computer science)">conceptual modeling</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ontology_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>[Ontology engineering] aims at making explicit the knowledge contained within software applications, and within enterprises and business procedures for a particular domain. Ontology engineering offers a direction towards solving the inter-operability problems brought about by semantic obstacles, i.e. the obstacles related to the definitions of business terms and software classes. Ontology engineering is a set of tasks related to the development of ontologies for a particular domain.</p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Line_Pouchard&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Line Pouchard (page does not exist)">Line Pouchard</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nenad_Ivezic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nenad Ivezic (page does not exist)">Nenad Ivezic</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Craig_Schlenoff&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Craig Schlenoff (page does not exist)">Craig Schlenoff</a>, Ontology Engineering for Distributed Collaboration in Manufacturing<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PIS00_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-PIS00-3">[3]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>Automated processing of information not interpretable by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Software_agents" title="Software agents">software agents</a> can be improved by adding rich <a href="/wiki/Semantics" title="Semantics">semantics</a> to the corresponding resources, such as video files. One of the approaches for the formal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conceptualization" title="Conceptualization">conceptualization</a> of represented <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knowledge_domain" title="Knowledge domain">knowledge domains</a> is the use of machine-interpretable ontologies, which provide <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Structured_data" title="Structured data">structured data</a> in, or based on, <a href="/wiki/Resource_Description_Framework" title="Resource Description Framework">RDF</a>, <a href="/wiki/RDF_Schema" title="RDF Schema">RDFS</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Web_Ontology_Language" title="Web Ontology Language">OWL</a>. Ontology engineering is the design and creation of such ontologies, which can contain more than just the list of terms (<a href="/wiki/Controlled_vocabulary" title="Controlled vocabulary">controlled vocabulary</a>); they contain terminological, assertional, and relational axioms to define concepts (classes), individuals, and roles (properties) (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TBox" title="TBox">TBox</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABox" title="ABox">ABox</a>, and RBox, respectively).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Ontology engineering is a relatively new field of study concerning the ontology development process, the ontology life cycle, the methods and methodologies for building ontologies,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PFC04_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-PFC04-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMN_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-DMN-6">[6]</a></sup> and the tool suites and languages that support them. A common way to provide the logical underpinning of ontologies is to formalize the axioms with <a href="/wiki/Description_logic" title="Description logic">description logics</a>, which can then be translated to <a href="/wiki/Resource_Description_Framework#Serialization_formats" title="Resource Description Framework">any serialization of RDF</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/RDF/XML" title="RDF/XML">RDF/XML</a> or <a href="/wiki/Turtle_(syntax)" title="Turtle (syntax)">Turtle</a>. Beyond the description logic axioms, ontologies might also contain <a href="/wiki/Semantic_Web_Rule_Language" title="Semantic Web Rule Language">SWRL</a> rules. The concept definitions can be mapped to any kind of resource or resource segment in <a href="/wiki/Resource_Description_Framework" title="Resource Description Framework">RDF</a>, such as images, videos, and <a href="/wiki/Region_of_interest" title="Region of interest">regions of interest</a>, to annotate objects, persons, etc., and interlink them with related resources across <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_base" title="Knowledge base">knowledge bases</a>, ontologies, and <a href="/wiki/Linked_data#Linked_open_data" title="Linked data">LOD</a> datasets. This information, based on human experience and knowledge, is valuable for <a href="/wiki/Semantic_reasoner" title="Semantic reasoner">reasoners</a> for the automated interpretation of sophisticated and ambiguous contents, such as the visual content of multimedia resources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Application areas of <a href="/wiki/Automated_reasoning" title="Automated reasoning">ontology-based reasoning</a> include, but are not limited to, <a href="/wiki/Information_retrieval" title="Information retrieval">information retrieval</a>, automated scene interpretation, and <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_extraction#Knowledge_discovery" title="Knowledge extraction">knowledge discovery</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ontology_languages">Ontology languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ontology_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ontology languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Ontology_language" title="Ontology language">ontology language</a></div>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Ontology_language" title="Ontology language">ontology language</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Formal_language" title="Formal language">formal language</a> used to encode the ontology. There are a number of such languages for ontologies, both proprietary and standards-based:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Common_logic" title="Common logic">Common logic</a> is ISO standard 24707, a specification for a family of ontology languages that can be accurately translated into each other.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Cyc" title="Cyc">Cyc</a> project has its own ontology language called <a href="/wiki/CycL" title="CycL">CycL</a>, based on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First-order_predicate_calculus" title="First-order predicate calculus">first-order predicate calculus</a> with some higher-order extensions.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Gellish" title="Gellish">Gellish</a> language includes rules for its own extension and thus integrates an ontology with an ontology language.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/IDEF5" title="IDEF5">IDEF5</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a> method to develop and maintain usable, accurate, domain ontologies.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Knowledge_Interchange_Format" title="Knowledge Interchange Format">KIF</a> is a syntax for <a href="/wiki/First-order_logic" title="First-order logic">first-order logic</a> that is based on <a href="/wiki/S-expression" title="S-expression">S-expressions</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rule_Interchange_Format" title="Rule Interchange Format">Rule Interchange Format</a> (RIF) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F-Logic" title="F-Logic">F-Logic</a> combine ontologies and rules.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Web_Ontology_Language" title="Web Ontology Language">OWL</a> is a language for making ontological statements, developed as a follow-on from <a href="/wiki/Resource_Description_Framework" title="Resource Description Framework">RDF</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RDFS" title="RDFS">RDFS</a>, as well as earlier ontology language projects including <a href="/wiki/Ontology_Inference_Layer" title="Ontology Inference Layer">OIL</a>, <a href="/wiki/DARPA_Agent_Markup_Language" title="DARPA Agent Markup Language">DAML</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DAMLplusOIL" title="DAMLplusOIL">DAML+OIL</a>. OWL is intended to be used over the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">World Wide Web</a>, and all its elements (classes, properties and individuals) are defined as RDF <a href="/wiki/Web_resource" title="Web resource">resources</a>, and identified by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uniform_resource_identifier" title="Uniform resource identifier">URIs</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/XBRL" title="XBRL">XBRL</a> (Extensible Business Reporting Language) is a syntax for expressing business semantics.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/OntoUML" title="OntoUML">OntoUML</a> is a well-founded language for specifying reference ontologies.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ontology_engineering_in_life_sciences">Ontology engineering in life sciences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ontology_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ontology engineering in life sciences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Life sciences is flourishing with ontologies that biologists use to make sense of their experiments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> For inferring correct conclusions from experiments, ontologies have to be structured optimally against the knowledge base they represent. The structure of an ontology needs to be changed continuously so that it is an accurate representation of the underlying <a href="/wiki/Domain_model" title="Domain model">domain</a>.</p>
<p>Recently, an automated method was introduced for engineering ontologies in life sciences such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gene_Ontology" title="Gene Ontology">Gene Ontology</a> (GO),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> one of the most successful and widely used biomedical ontology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Based on information theory, it restructures ontologies so that the levels represent the desired specificity of the concepts. Similar information theoretic approaches have also been used for optimal partition of Gene Ontology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Given the mathematical nature of such engineering <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Algorithms" title="Algorithms">algorithms</a>, these optimizations can be automated to produce a principled and scalable architecture to restructure ontologies such as GO.</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Open_Biomedical_Ontologies" title="Open Biomedical Ontologies">Open Biomedical Ontologies</a></b> (OBO), a 2006 initiative of the U.S. National Center for Biomedical Ontology, that provides a common 'foundry' for various ontology initiatives, amongst which are:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Generic_Model_Organism_Database" title="Generic Model Organism Database">Generic Model Organism Project</a> (GMOD)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gene_Ontology" title="Gene Ontology">Gene Ontology</a> Consortium</li>
<li>Sequence Ontology</li>
<li>Ontology Lookup Service</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Plant_ontology" title="Plant ontology">Plant Ontology</a> Consortium</li>
<li>Standards and Ontologies for Functional Genomics</li>
</ul>
<p>and more</p>
