<p><b>Building design</b> refers to the broadly based <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architectural</a>, <a href="/wiki/Architectural_engineering" title="Architectural engineering">engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technical</a> applications to the <a href="/wiki/Design" title="Design">design</a> of <a href="/wiki/Building" title="Building">buildings</a>. All building projects require the services of a building designer, typically a licensed <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> or <a href="/wiki/Structural_engineer" title="Structural engineer">structural engineer</a>. Smaller, less complicated projects often do not require a licensed professional, and the design of such projects is often undertaken by building designers, draftspersons, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interior_designers" title="Interior designers">interior designers</a> (for interior fit-outs or renovations), or <a href="/wiki/General_contractor" title="General contractor">contractors</a>. Larger, more complex building projects require the services of many professionals trained in specialist disciplines, usually coordinated by an architect.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Occupations">Occupations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Building_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Occupations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Architect">Architect</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Building_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architect">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">Architect</a></div>
<p>An architect is a person trained in the planning, design and supervision of the construction of buildings. Professionally, an architect's decisions affect public safety, and thus an architect must undergo specialized training consisting of advanced education and a practicum (or internship) for practical experience to earn a license to practice architecture. In most of the world's jurisdictions, the professional and commercial use of the term "architect" is legally protected.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Building_engineers">Building engineers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Building_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Building engineers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Building_engineering" title="Building engineering">Building engineering</a></div>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Building_engineering" title="Building engineering">Building engineering</a> typically includes the services of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_engineers" title="Electrical engineers">electrical</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineers" title="Mechanical engineers">mechanical</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Structural_engineers" title="Structural engineers">Structural engineers</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Draftsperson_.28Draughtsman.29">Draftsperson (Draughtsman)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Building_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Draftsperson (Draughtsman)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Technical_drawing" title="Technical drawing">draftsperson</a> or <a href="/wiki/Documentation" title="Documentation">documenter</a> is someone who has attained a certificate or <a href="/wiki/Diploma" title="Diploma">diploma</a> in architectural drafting (or equivalent training), and provides services relating to the preparation of <a href="/wiki/Technical_drawing" title="Technical drawing">construction documents</a> rather than building design. Some draftspersons are employed by architectural design firms and building contractors, while others are self-employed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Building_designer">Building designer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Building_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Building designer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In many places, <a href="/wiki/Building_code" title="Building code">building codes</a> and legislation of professions allow persons to design single family residential buildings and in some cases light commercial buildings without an architectural license. As such, "Building designer" is a common designation in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and elsewhere for someone who offers building design services but is not a licensed architect or engineer.</p>
<p>Anyone may use the title of "building designer" in the broadest sense. In many places, a building designer may achieve certification demonstrating a higher level of training. In the U.S., the <i>National Council of Building Designer Certification</i> (NCBDC),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> an offshoot of the <i>American Institute of Building Design</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> administers a program leading to the title of <i>Certified Professional Building Designer</i> (CPBD). In most cases, building designers are trained as <a href="/wiki/Architectural_technologist" title="Architectural technologist">architectural technologists</a> or draftspersons; they may also be architecture school graduates that have not completed licensing requirements.</p>
<p>Many building designers are known as "residential" or "home designers", since they focus mainly on residential design and remodeling. In the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>, "Residential Designer" is a regulated term for those who are registered as such under Nevada State Board of Architecture, Interior Design and Residential Design, and one may not legally represent oneself in a professional capacity without being currently registered.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> where use of the term architect and some derivatives is highly restricted but the architectural design of buildings has very few restrictions in place, the term building designer is used extensively by people or design practices who are not registered by the relevant State Board of Architects. In <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a> the term building design is used in legislation which licences practitioners as part of a broader building industry licensing system. In <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a> there is a registration process for building designers and in other States there is currently no regulation of the profession. A Building Designers Association operates in each state to represent the interests of building designers.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Building_Surveyor_.28UK.29">Building Surveyor (UK)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Building_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Building Surveyor (UK)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Chartered_Building_Surveyor" title="Chartered Building Surveyor">Building surveyors</a> are technically minded general practitioners in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and elsewhere,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> trained much like <a href="/wiki/Architectural_technologist" title="Architectural technologist">architectural technologists</a>. In the UK, the knowledge and expertise of the building surveyor is applied to various tasks in the property and construction markets, including building design for smaller residential and light commercial projects. This aspect of the practice is similar to other European occupations, most notably the <i>geometra</i> in Italy, but also the <i>géomètre</i> in France, Belgium and Switzerland. the building surveyors are also capable on establishment of bills of quantities for the new works and renovation or maintenance or rehabilitation works.</p>
<p>The profession of Building Surveyor does not exist in the US. The title <i>Surveyor</i> refers almost exclusively to <a href="/wiki/Surveying" title="Surveying">Land surveyors</a>. <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">Architects</a>, Building Designers, Residential Designers, Construction Managers, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_Inspector" title="Home Inspector">Home Inspectors</a> perform some or all of the work of the U.K. Building Surveyor.</p>
