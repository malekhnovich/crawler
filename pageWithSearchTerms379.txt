<p><b>Applied science</b> is a discipline of <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> that applies existing scientific knowledge to develop more practical applications, like <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a> or inventions.</p>
<p>Within <a href="/wiki/Natural_science" title="Natural science">natural science</a>, disciplines that are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basic_science" title="Basic science">basic science</a>, also called pure science, develop <i>information</i> to predict and perhaps explain—thus somehow understand—phenomena in the natural world. Applied science applies science to real world practice. This includes a broad range of science fields from <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Child_Care" title="Child Care">Child Care</a>.</p>
<p>Applied science can also apply <a href="/wiki/Formal_science" title="Formal science">formal science</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">statistics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Probability_theory" title="Probability theory">probability theory</a>, as in <a href="/wiki/Epidemiology" title="Epidemiology">epidemiology</a>. <a href="/wiki/Genetic_epidemiology" title="Genetic epidemiology">Genetic epidemiology</a> is an applied science applying both biological and statistical methods.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Branches_of_applied_science">Branches of applied science</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Branches of applied science">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote outlinearticle" role="note">For a topical guide to this subject, see <a href="/wiki/Outline_of_applied_science#Branches_of_applied_science" title="Outline of applied science">Outline of applied science#Branches of applied science</a>.</div>
<p>Engineering sciences include <a href="/wiki/Thermodynamics" title="Thermodynamics">thermodynamics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heat_transfer" title="Heat transfer">heat transfer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fluid_mechanics" title="Fluid mechanics">fluid mechanics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Statics" title="Statics">statics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dynamics_(mechanics)" title="Dynamics (mechanics)">dynamics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mechanics_of_materials" title="Mechanics of materials">mechanics of materials</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kinematics" title="Kinematics">kinematics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetism" title="Electromagnetism">electromagnetism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Materials_science" title="Materials science">materials science</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth_sciences" title="Earth sciences">earth sciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering_physics" title="Engineering physics">engineering physics</a>.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical_sciences" title="Medical sciences">Medical sciences</a>, for instance <a href="/wiki/Medical_microbiology" title="Medical microbiology">medical microbiology</a> and its <a href="/wiki/Clinical_virology" title="Clinical virology">clinical virology</a>, are applied sciences that apply <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a> toward medical knowledge and inventions, but not necessarily medical technology, whose development is more specifically <a href="/wiki/Biomedicine" title="Biomedicine">biomedicine</a> or <a href="/wiki/Biomedical_engineering" title="Biomedical engineering">biomedical engineering</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_education">In education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Education_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Education in the United Kingdom">the United Kingdom's educational system</a>, Applied Science refers to a suite of "vocational" science qualifications that run alongside "traditional" <a href="/wiki/General_Certificate_of_Secondary_Education" title="General Certificate of Secondary Education">General Certificate of Secondary Education</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A-Level" title="A-Level">A-Level</a> Sciences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Applied Science courses generally contain more coursework (also known as portfolio or internally assessed work) compared to their traditional counterparts. These are an evolution of the GNVQ qualifications that were offered up to 2005 These courses regularly come under scrutiny and are due for review following the Wolf Report 2011;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> however, their merits are argued elsewhere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the United States, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_College_of_William_%26_Mary" title="The College of William &amp; Mary">The College of William &amp; Mary</a> offers an <a href="/wiki/Minor_(academic)" title="Minor (academic)">undergraduate minor</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">Master of Science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Doctor of Philosophy</a> degrees in "applied science." Courses and research cover varied fields including <a href="/wiki/Neuroscience" title="Neuroscience">neuroscience</a>, <a href="/wiki/Optics" title="Optics">optics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Materials_science_and_engineering" title="Materials science and engineering">materials science and engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nondestructive_testing" title="Nondestructive testing">nondestructive testing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_magnetic_resonance" title="Nuclear magnetic resonance">nuclear magnetic resonance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In New York City, the Bloomberg administration awarded the consortium of Cornell-Technion $100 million in City capital to construct the universities' proposed Applied Sciences campus on Roosevelt Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
