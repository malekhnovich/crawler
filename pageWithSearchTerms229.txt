<p>The following <a href="/wiki/Outline_(list)" title="Outline (list)">outline</a> is provided as an overview of and topical guide to software engineering:</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">Software engineering</a></b> – application of a systematic, disciplined, quantifiable approach to the development, operation, and maintenance of <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a>; that is the application of <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> to <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technologies_and_practices">Technologies and practices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Outline_of_software_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Technologies and practices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Skilled <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a> engineers use technologies and practices from a variety of fields to improve their productivity in creating software and to improve the quality of the delivered product.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Software_applications">Software applications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Outline_of_software_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Software applications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">Software</a> engineers build <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a> (<a href="/wiki/Application_software" title="Application software">applications</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operating_systems" title="Operating systems">operating systems</a>, <a href="/wiki/System_software" title="System software">system software</a>) that people use.</p>
<p>Applications influence software engineering by pressuring developers to solve problems in new ways. For example, consumer software emphasizes low cost, medical software emphasizes high quality, and Internet commerce software emphasizes rapid development.</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a> All traditional engineering branches use software extensively. Engineers use spreadsheets, more than they ever used calculators. Engineers use custom software <a href="/wiki/Tool" title="Tool">tools</a> to design, analyze, and simulate their own projects, like bridges and power lines. These projects resemble software in many respects, because the work exists as <a href="/wiki/Electronic_document" title="Electronic document">electronic documents</a> and goes through analysis, <a href="/wiki/Design" title="Design">design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Implementation" title="Implementation">implementation</a>, and testing phases. Software tools for engineers use the tenets of computer science; as well as the tenets of calculus, physics, and chemistry.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Disasters">Disasters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Outline_of_software_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Disasters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Software has played a role in many high-profile disasters.</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ariane_5_Flight_501" title="Ariane 5 Flight 501">Ariane 5 Flight 501</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mars_Climate_Orbiter" title="Mars Climate Orbiter">Mars probe</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Denver_International_Airport" title="Denver International Airport">Denver International Airport</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TAURUS_(share_trading)" title="TAURUS (share trading)">TAURUS</a> — UK share <a href="/wiki/Settlement_(finance)" title="Settlement (finance)">settlement</a> system and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dematerialised&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dematerialised (page does not exist)">dematerialised</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Central_share_depository&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Central share depository (page does not exist)">central share depository</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Therac-25" title="Therac-25">Therac-25</a> — A radiation therapy machine responsible for six overdoses due to faulty software.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Airbus_A320" title="Airbus A320">Airbus A320</a> —<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Airbus_A320" title="Airbus A320">Airbus A320</a>, while was controversial in software engineering circles, being the first civilian <a href="/wiki/Fly-by-wire" title="Fly-by-wire">Fly-by-wire</a> aircraft. In the Airbus flight control systems, the computer has the final say on all decisions, meaning the safety of passengers depends upon the accuracy of the software specification, and the competence of the engineering teams producing the (multiple, independent) software stacks. The <a href="/wiki/Air_Inter_Flight_148" title="Air Inter Flight 148">Strasbourg A320 crash of Jan 21, 1992</a> is partially related to software in that poor user interface design was a contributing factor.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patriot_missile" title="Patriot missile">Failure at Dharan</a> — Patriot Missile clock issue.</li>
</ul>
