<p><b>Kenexa, an IBM Company</b> provides <a href="/wiki/Employment" title="Employment">employment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Employee_retention" title="Employee retention">retention</a> solutions to assist organizations in hiring and keeping workers. It offers <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recruitment_process_outsourcing" title="Recruitment process outsourcing">recruitment process outsourcing</a> (RPO) (which assists in global recruiting);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Onboarding" title="Onboarding">onboarding</a> solutions (form management for <a href="/wiki/Electronic_signature" title="Electronic signature">electronic signature</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legal_documents" title="Legal documents">legal documents</a> and <a href="/wiki/Workflow" title="Workflow">workflow</a> tracking); employee assessment, abilities assessment for emplyoment candidates (Kenexa Prove It<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>); and Kenexa <a href="/wiki/Interview" title="Interview">Interview</a> Builder, a structured <a href="/wiki/Job_interview" title="Job interview">interview</a> <a href="/wiki/Archive" title="Archive">archive</a> with example questions.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenexa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1987, Kenexa began by providing <a href="/wiki/Employment_agency" title="Employment agency">recruitment services</a>. Within the first decade of operation, the firm creating its first automated recruiting management system, and provided <a href="/wiki/Employee_research" title="Employee research">employee research</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Employee_performance_management" title="Employee performance management">employee performance management</a> integrated with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/On-demand_software" title="On-demand software">on-demand software</a>.</p>
<p>By 1998, the company had purchased fifteen organizations, allowing them to offer on-demand <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_capital_management" title="Human capital management">human capital management</a> services.During the next 3 years, the firm acquired four additional companies and thus was able to offer employee <a href="/wiki/Screening_(economics)" title="Screening (economics)">screening</a>, behavioral assessment and skills testing products.</p>
<p>In 2005, the company realized its <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> followed by the acquisition of BrassRing LLC, Gantz Wiley Research, Knowledge Workers Inc., Psychometric Services and Webhire, which preceded the purchase of HRC Human Resources Consulting GmbH and StraightSource in 2007, which expanded their business in Europe.</p>
<p>From 2008-2009, Kenexa expanded to 30 offices in Asia, Europe, the Middle East and North America. The company also took control of Quorum International Search Limited. This was followed in 2010 by Kenexa's acquisitions of the Centre for High Performance Development and Salary.com.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 27, 2012, it was announced that Kenexa had been acquired by <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> for $1.3 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The acquisition was closed on December 4, 2012 and Kenexa became known as <b>Kenexa, an IBM Company</b> with approximately 2,800 Kenexa employees in 21 countries joining IBM.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p><b>Brassring LLC</b>, Founded in 1999 in <a href="/wiki/Waltham,_Massachusetts" title="Waltham, Massachusetts">Waltham, Massachusetts</a>, is a provider of candidate searching, hiring strategy, skills management and outsourcing software solutions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <b>Centre for High Performance Development</b> , founded in 1996 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London,_England" title="London, England">London, England</a>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Management_training" title="Management training">management training</a> company that offers <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_resources_consulting" title="Human resources consulting">human resources consultancy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leadership_development" title="Leadership development">leadership development</a> and management training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Gantz Wiley Research</b>, founded in 1986, and located in Minneapolis, Minnesota, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Survey_data" title="Survey data">survey data</a> <a href="/wiki/Marketing_research" title="Marketing research">analysis</a> firm which conducts <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Statistical_survey" title="Statistical survey">surveys</a> of employees and clients for customers ranging from <a href="/wiki/Bank" title="Bank">banks</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Discount_stores" title="Discount stores">discount stores</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hotstocked1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-hotstocked1-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The company was responsible for creating the WorkTrends database of <a href="/wiki/Employee_surveys" title="Employee surveys">employee survey</a> results, which looked at subjects such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Customer_relations" title="Customer relations">customer relations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Employee_engagement" title="Employee engagement">employee engagement</a>, and leadership.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hotstocked1_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-hotstocked1-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Knowledge Workers, Inc.</b> of <a href="/wiki/Englewood,_Colorado" title="Englewood, Colorado">Englewood, Colorado</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> founded in 1985, supplies technology assistance and <a href="/wiki/Human_resource_consulting" title="Human resource consulting">human resource consulting</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_agencies" title="Government agencies">government agencies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Salary.com, Inc.</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Compensation_of_employees" title="Compensation of employees">compensation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_resource_management" title="Human resource management">human resource management</a> solutions company in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Human_capital" title="Human capital">human capital</a> <a href="/wiki/Software_as_a_service" title="Software as a service">software as a service</a> (SaaS) market. The company's services are used by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compensation_professional" title="Compensation professional">compensation professionals</a> for compensation planning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<ul>
<li>Top Five Global <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recruitment_Process_Outsourcing" title="Recruitment Process Outsourcing">Recruitment Process Outsourcing</a> (RPO) Vendor (Baker's Dozen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Customer_Satisfaction" title="Customer Satisfaction">Customer Satisfaction</a> Ratings 2010)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>

<li>Human Resources Magazine, Top Product of the Year, 2008, 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
