<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Blackboard (design pattern)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a>, the <b>blackboard pattern</b> provides a computational framework for the design and implementation of systems that used to integrate large and diverse specialized modules, and implement complex, non deterministic control strategies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:tcpbms_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:tcpbms-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:m_bdp_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:m_bdp-2">[2]</a></sup> Blackboard pattern comes under behavioral design pattern.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:m_bdp_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:m_bdp-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>This pattern has been identified by the members of the HEARSAY-II project and first applied for speech recognition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:tcpbms_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:tcpbms-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackboard_(design_pattern)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>the blackboard model defines three main components:</p>
<ul>
<li>blackboard - a structured global memory containing objects from the solution space</li>
<li>knowledge sources - highly specialized modules with their own representation</li>
<li>control component - selects, configures and execute knowledge sources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:tcpbms_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-:tcpbms-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Implementation">Implementation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackboard_(design_pattern)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Implementation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>First step is to design the solution space (i.e. various solutions) which leads to the definition of blackboard structure. Then, knowledge source are to be identified. These two activities are very related.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:tcpbms_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-:tcpbms-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The next step is to specify the control component which is generally in the form of a complex scheduler that makes use of a set of domain-specific heuristics to rate the relevance of executable knowledge sources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:tcpbms_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-:tcpbms-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<div class="center">
<div class="floatnone"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Blackboad_pattern_system_structure.png" title="System Structure"><img alt="System Structure" data-file-height="229" data-file-width="470" height="229" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/18/Blackboad_pattern_system_structure.png" width="470"/></a></div>
</div>
<p><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:tcpbms_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-:tcpbms-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Known_Uses">Known Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackboard_(design_pattern)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Known Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some usage-domains are:</p>
<ul>
<li>speech recognition</li>
<li>vehicle identification and tracking</li>
<li>identification of the structure of protein molecules</li>
<li>sonar signals interpretation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:tcpbms_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-:tcpbms-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Consequences">Consequences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackboard_(design_pattern)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Consequences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The blackboard pattern provides effective solutions for designing and implementing complex systems where heterogeneous modules have to be dynamically combined to solve a problem. This provides properties such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>reusability</li>
<li>changeability</li>
<li>robustness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:tcpbms_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-:tcpbms-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Blackboard pattern allows multiple processes to work closer together on separate threads, polling, and reacting if it is needed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:m_bdp_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-:m_bdp-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
