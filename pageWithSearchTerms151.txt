<p><b>The Hillside Group</b> is an educational <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit_organization" title="Nonprofit organization">nonprofit organization</a> founded in August 1993<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to help <a href="/wiki/Software_developer" title="Software developer">software developers</a> analyze and document common development and design problems as <a href="/wiki/Software_design_pattern" title="Software design pattern">software design patterns</a>. The Hillside Group supports the patterns community through sponsorship of the <a href="/wiki/Pattern_Languages_of_Programs" title="Pattern Languages of Programs">Pattern Languages of Programs</a> conferences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hillside_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In August 1993, Kent Beck and Grady Booch sponsored a mountain retreat in Colorado where a group converged on foundations for software patterns. Ward Cunningham, Ralph Johnson, Ken Auer, Hal Hildebrand, Grady Booch, Kent Beck and Jim Coplien struggled with Alexander's ideas and our own experiences to forge a marriage of objects and patterns. The Group agreed that we were ready to build on Erich Gamma's foundation work studying object-oriented patterns, to use patterns in a generative way in the sense that Christopher Alexander uses patterns for urban planning and building architecture. We then used the term <i>generative</i> to mean <i>creational</i> to distinguish them from <i>Gamma patterns</i> that captured observations. The Group was meeting on the side of a hill when all this occurred, hence the name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since then, the Hillside Group has been incorporated as an educational non-profit. It currently sponsors and helps run various PLoP (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pattern_Languages_of_Programming" title="Pattern Languages of Programming">Pattern Languages of Programming</a>) conferences<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> such as <a class="external text" href="http://www.hillside.net/guruplop/" rel="nofollow">GuruPLoP</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.hillside.net/plop/" rel="nofollow">PLoP</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.europlop.net/" rel="nofollow">EuroPlop</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://hillside.net/chiliplop/" rel="nofollow">ChiliPlop</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.hillside.net/guruplop/" rel="nofollow">GuruPLoP</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://patterns-wg.fuka.info.waseda.ac.jp/asianplop/" rel="nofollow">Asian PLoP</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.scrumplop.org/" rel="nofollow">Scrum PLoP</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.vikingplop.org/" rel="nofollow">Viking PLoP</a> and <a class="external text" href="http://www.dimap.ufrn.br/sugarloafplop2012/" rel="nofollow">Sugarloaf PLoP</a>. Following are a list of historical PLoP conferences sponsored by The Hillside Group: ParaPLoP, KoalaPLoP, Mensore PLoP, <a class="external text" href="http://illinois.edu/calendar/detail/2654?eventId=788652&amp;calMin=201205&amp;cal=20110510&amp;skinId=2864" rel="nofollow">ParaPLoP</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://metaplop.org/" rel="nofollow">Meta PLoP</a> and UP97) The Hillside Group has also been responsible for getting the Pattern Languages Of Program Design series of books put together and published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hillside_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Hillside Group sponsors the Pattern Languages of Programs conferences in various countries, including the U.S., Brazil, Norway, Germany, Australia, and Japan. The Hillside Group assisted in publishing the Pattern Languages of Program Design book series until 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Since 2006, The Hillside Group has published patterns and conference proceedings through the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a> (ACM) Digital Library.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Patterns_Library">Patterns Library</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hillside_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Patterns Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="external text" href="http://hillside.net/patterns/" rel="nofollow">Hillside Patterns Library</a> contains a comprehensive archive of patterns developed by the community, either directly or indirectly through the PLoP conferences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conferences">Conferences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hillside_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conferences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Hillside Group sponsors the conferences listed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The conferences focus on writing patterns, workshops, and invited talks related to pattern development. Most of the conferences are held annually and encourage attendees to submit papers pre-conference for inclusion in the writer's workshops. The papers undergo a shepherding process, where they are analyzed and evolved before conference attendance.</p>
<ul>
<li>PLoP: <a href="/wiki/Pattern_Languages_of_Programs" title="Pattern Languages of Programs">Pattern Languages of Programs</a></li>
<li>ChiliPLoP: Southwestern Conference on Pattern Languages of Programs <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.hillside.net/chiliplop" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></li>
<li>EuroPLoP: European Conference on Pattern Languages of Programs <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.hillside.net/europlop" rel="nofollow">[2]</a></li>
<li>AsianPLoP: Japanese Conference on Pattern Languages of Programs <a class="external autonumber" href="http://patterns-wg.fuka.info.waseda.ac.jp/asianplop/" rel="nofollow">[3]</a></li>
<li>SugarLoafPLoP: Latin American Conference on Pattern Languages of Programming <a class="external autonumber" href="http://hillside.net/conferences/sugarloaf-plop" rel="nofollow">[4]</a></li>
<li>VikingPLoP: Nordic Conference on Pattern Languages of Programs <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.vikingplop.org/" rel="nofollow">[5]</a></li>
<li>ScrumPLoP: Conference on Pattern Languages of Scrum <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.scrumplop.org/" rel="nofollow">[6]</a></li>
<li>EduPLoP: Educational Patterns Writing Workshop <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.eduplop.net/" rel="nofollow">[7]</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Hillside_Group_Board">The Hillside Group Board</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hillside_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Hillside Group Board">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The President of the The Hillside Group for 2010–2014 is <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Yoder_(computer_scientist)" title="Joseph Yoder (computer scientist)">Joseph Yoder</a> of The Refactory, Inc.</p>
<p>The Hillside Group is led by a Board consisting of the President, Vice-President, Chief Operating Officer, Treasurer, two Directors, Secretary, two Editors in Chief and four Members.</p>
