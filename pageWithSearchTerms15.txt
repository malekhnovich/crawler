<p><b>Howard G.</b> "<b>Ward</b>" <b>Cunningham</b> (born May 26, 1949<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Programmer" title="Programmer">computer programmer</a> who developed the first <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">wiki</a>. A pioneer in both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Design_pattern_(computer_science)" title="Design pattern (computer science)">design patterns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Extreme_programming" title="Extreme programming">extreme programming</a>, he started programming the software <a href="/wiki/WikiWikiWeb" title="WikiWikiWeb">WikiWikiWeb</a> in 1994 and installed it on the website of his software consultancy, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cunningham_%26_Cunningham" title="Cunningham &amp; Cunningham">Cunningham &amp; Cunningham</a> (commonly known by its domain name, <b>c2.com</b>), on March 25, 1995, as an add-on to the <a href="/wiki/Portland_Pattern_Repository" title="Portland Pattern Repository">Portland Pattern Repository</a>.</p>
<p>He currently lives in <a href="/wiki/Beaverton,_Oregon" title="Beaverton, Oregon">Beaverton, Oregon</a>, and is a programmer at <a href="/wiki/New_Relic" title="New Relic">New Relic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Previously he was the Co-Creation Czar for CitizenGlobal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He is <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a>'s first Code for a Better World Fellow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He has authored a book about wikis, titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wiki_Way" title="The Wiki Way">The Wiki Way</a></i>, and also invented <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Framework_for_Integrated_Tests" title="Framework for Integrated Tests">Framework for Integrated Tests</a>. He was a keynote speaker at the first three instances of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WikiSym" title="WikiSym">WikiSym</a> conference series on <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">wiki</a> research and practice.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_history">Personal history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ward_Cunningham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cunningham was born in <a href="/wiki/Michigan_City,_Indiana" title="Michigan City, Indiana">Michigan City, Indiana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He received his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's degree</a> in interdisciplinary engineering (<a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>) and his master's degree in computer science from <a href="/wiki/Purdue_University" title="Purdue University">Purdue University</a>. He is a founder of Cunningham &amp; Cunningham, Inc. He has also served as Director of R&amp;D at Wyatt Software and as Principal Engineer in the <a href="/wiki/Tektronix" title="Tektronix">Tektronix</a> Computer Research Laboratory. He is founder of <a href="/wiki/The_Hillside_Group" title="The Hillside Group">The Hillside Group</a> and has served as program chair of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pattern_Languages_of_Programming" title="Pattern Languages of Programming">Pattern Languages of Programming</a> conference which it sponsors. Cunningham was part of the <a href="/wiki/Smalltalk" title="Smalltalk">Smalltalk</a> community. From December 2003 until October 2005, he worked for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microsoft_Corporation" title="Microsoft Corporation">Microsoft Corporation</a> in the "patterns &amp; practices" group. From October 2005 to May 2007, he held the position of Director of Committer Community Development at the <a href="/wiki/Eclipse_Foundation" title="Eclipse Foundation">Eclipse Foundation</a>.</p>
<p>In May 2009, Cunningham joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AboutUs.org" title="AboutUs.org">AboutUs</a> as its <a href="/wiki/Chief_technology_officer" title="Chief technology officer">chief technology officer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On March 24, 2011 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oregonian" title="The Oregonian">The Oregonian</a></i> reported that Cunningham had quietly departed AboutUs to join Venice-based CitizenGlobal, a startup working on crowd-sourced video content, as their chief technology officer. He remains "an adviser" with AboutUs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2014, Cunningham resides in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>Cunningham is well known for a few widely disseminated ideas which he originated and developed. The most famous among these are the <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">wiki</a> and many ideas in the field of <a href="/wiki/Software_design_pattern" title="Software design pattern">software design patterns</a>, initiated by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gang_of_Four_(software)" title="Gang of Four (software)">Gang of Four</a></i> (GoF). He owns the company Cunningham &amp; Cunningham Inc., a consultancy that has specialized in object-oriented programming. He also created the site (and software) <a href="/wiki/WikiWikiWeb" title="WikiWikiWeb">WikiWikiWeb</a>, the first internet wiki.</p>
<p>When asked in a 2006 interview with internetnews.com whether he considered patenting the wiki concept, he explained that he thought the idea "just sounded like something that no one would want to pay money for."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cunningham is interested in tracking the number and location of wiki page edits as a sociological experiment and may even consider the degradation of a wiki page as part of its process to stability. "There are those who give and those who take. You can tell by reading what they write."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Steven_McGeady" title="Steven McGeady">Steven McGeady</a>, Cunningham advised him in the early 1980s, "The best way to get the right answer on the Internet is not to ask a question, it's to post the wrong answer." McGeady dubbed this <a class="extiw" href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Cunningham%27s_Law" title="meta:Cunningham's Law">Cunningham's law</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Although Cunningham was referring to interactions on <a href="/wiki/Usenet" title="Usenet">Usenet</a>, the law has been used to describe how Wikipedia works.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Cunningham created <a href="/wiki/Smallest_Federated_Wiki" title="Smallest Federated Wiki">Smallest Federated Wiki</a>, a tool for wiki federation, which applies aspects of software development such as forking to wiki pages.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Patterns_and_extreme_programming">Patterns and extreme programming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ward_Cunningham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Patterns and extreme programming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ward Cunningham has contributed to the practice of <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_programming" title="Object-oriented programming">object-oriented programming</a>, in particular the use of <a href="/wiki/Pattern_language" title="Pattern language">pattern languages</a> and (with <a href="/wiki/Kent_Beck" title="Kent Beck">Kent Beck</a>) the <a href="/wiki/Class-responsibility-collaboration_card" title="Class-responsibility-collaboration card">class-responsibility-collaboration cards</a>. He also contributes to the <a href="/wiki/Extreme_programming" title="Extreme programming">extreme programming</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Software_development_methodology" title="Software development methodology">software development methodology</a>. Much of this work was done collaboratively on the first wiki site.</p>
