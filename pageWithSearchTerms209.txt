<p>Software Requirements is a field within <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a> that deals with establishing the needs of stakeholders that are to be solved by software. The IEEE Standard Glossary of Software Engineering Terminology defines a requirement as:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ol>
<li>A condition or capability needed by a user to solve a problem or achieve an objective.</li>
<li>A condition or capability that must be met or possessed by a system or system component to satisfy a contract, standard, specification, or other formally imposed document.</li>
<li>A documented representation of a condition or capability as in 1 or 2.</li>
</ol>
<p>The activities related to working with software requirements can broadly be broken up into Elicitation, Analysis, Specification, and Management.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Elicitation">Elicitation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_requirements&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Elicitation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Requirements_elicitation" title="Requirements elicitation">Requirements elicitation</a></div>
<p>Elicitation is the gathering and discovery of requirements from stakeholders and other sources. A variety of techniques can be used such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joint_application_design" title="Joint application design">joint application design</a> (JAD) sessions, interviews, document analysis, focus groups, etc. Elicitation is the first step of requirements development.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Analysis">Analysis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_requirements&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Analysis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Requirements_analysis" title="Requirements analysis">Requirements analysis</a></div>
<p>Analysis is the logical breakdown that proceeds from elicitation. Analysis involves reaching a richer and more precise understanding of each requirement and representing sets of requirements in multiple, complementary ways.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specification">Specification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_requirements&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Specification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Software_requirements_specification" title="Software requirements specification">Software requirements specification</a></div>
<p>Specification involves representing and storing the collected requirements knowledge in a persistent and well-organized fashion that facilitates effective communication and change management. Use cases, user stories, functional requirements, and visual analysis models are popular choices for requirements specification.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Validation">Validation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_requirements&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Validation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Validation involves techniques to confirm that the correct set of requirements has been specified to build a solution that satisfies the project's business objectives.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_requirements&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Requirements_management" title="Requirements management">Requirements management</a></div>
<p>Requirements change during projects and there are often many of them. Management of this change becomes paramount to ensuring that the correct software is built for the stakeholders.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tool_support_for_requirements_engineering">Tool support for requirements engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_requirements&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tool support for requirements engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Specialized commercial tools for requirements engineering are 3SL Cradle, <a href="/wiki/IRise" title="IRise">IRise</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=GatherSpace_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="GatherSpace (company) (page does not exist)">Gatherspace</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rational_RequisitePro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rational RequisitePro (page does not exist)">Rational RequisitePro</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IBM_Rational_DOORS" title="IBM Rational DOORS">Doors</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CaliberRM&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CaliberRM (page does not exist)">CaliberRM</a> or <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=QFDCapture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="QFDCapture (page does not exist)">QFDCapture</a>, but also free tools like <a href="/wiki/FreeMind" title="FreeMind">FreeMind</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://reqchecker.eu/" rel="nofollow">Reqchecker</a> together with MS Office, <a href="/wiki/Concordion" title="Concordion">Concordion</a> can be used.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Issue trackers implementing the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Volere&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Volere (page does not exist)">Volere</a> requirements template have been used successfully in distributed environments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
