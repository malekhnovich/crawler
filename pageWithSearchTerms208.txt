<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Software Engineering Body of Knowledge</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p>The <i><b>Software Engineering Body of Knowledge</b></i> (<b>SWEBOK</b>) is an international standard ISO/IEC TR 19759:2005<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> specifying a guide to the generally accepted Software Engineering Body of Knowledge.</p>
<p>The Guide to the Software Engineering Body of Knowledge (SWEBOK Guide) has been created through cooperation among several professional bodies and members of industry and is published by the <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Computer_Society" title="IEEE Computer Society">IEEE Computer Society</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IEEE" title="IEEE">IEEE</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The standard can be accessed freely from the <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Computer_Society" title="IEEE Computer Society">IEEE Computer Society</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In late 2013, SWEBOK V3 was approved for publication and released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="SWEBOK_Version_3">SWEBOK Version 3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_Engineering_Body_of_Knowledge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: SWEBOK Version 3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The published version of SWEBOK V3 has the following 15 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knowledge_area" title="Knowledge area">knowledge areas</a> (KAs) within the field of <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a>:</p>
<ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/Software_design" title="Software design">Software design</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_construction" title="Software construction">Software construction</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_testing" title="Software testing">Software testing</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_maintenance" title="Software maintenance">Software maintenance</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_configuration_management" title="Software configuration management">Software configuration management</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Software_engineering_management" title="Software engineering management">Software engineering management</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Software_engineering_process" title="Software engineering process">Software engineering process</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">Software engineering</a> models and methods</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_quality" title="Software quality">Software quality</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Software_engineering_professional_practice" title="Software engineering professional practice">Software engineering professional practice</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Software_engineering_economics" title="Software engineering economics">Software engineering economics</a></li>
<li>Computing foundations</li>
<li>Mathematical foundations</li>
<li>Engineering foundations</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_Edition_of_the_SWEBOK">2004 Edition of the SWEBOK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_Engineering_Body_of_Knowledge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2004 Edition of the SWEBOK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li><a href="/wiki/Software_testing" title="Software testing">Software testing</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_maintenance" title="Software maintenance">Software maintenance</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_configuration_management" title="Software configuration management">Software configuration management</a></li>
<li>Software engineering management (<a href="/wiki/Engineering_management" title="Engineering management">Engineering management</a>)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Software_engineering_process" title="Software engineering process">Software engineering process</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineering_tools_and_methods&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Software engineering tools and methods (page does not exist)">Software engineering tools and methods</a></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Similar_Efforts">Similar Efforts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_Engineering_Body_of_Knowledge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Similar Efforts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A similar effort to define a body of knowledge for software engineering is the "Computing Curriculum Software Engineering (CCSE)," officially named <a href="/wiki/Software_Engineering_2004" title="Software Engineering 2004">Software Engineering 2004</a> (SE2004). The curriculum largely overlaps with the 2004 SWEBOK V2 because the SWEBOK has been used as one of its sources; however, it is more directed towards academia. Whereas the SWEBOK Guide defines the <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a> knowledge that practitioners should have after four years of practice, SE2004 defines the knowledge that an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undergraduate" title="Undergraduate">undergraduate</a> software engineering student should possess upon graduation (including knowledge of mathematics, general engineering principles, and other related areas). SWEBOK V3 aims to address these intersections.</p>
