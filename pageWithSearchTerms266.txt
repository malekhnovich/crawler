<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">ISO/IEC 15504</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>ISO/IEC 15504</b> <i>Information technology – Process assessment</i>, also termed <b>Software Process Improvement and Capability Determination</b> (<b>SPICE</b>), is a set of <a href="/wiki/Technical_standard" title="Technical standard">technical standards</a> documents for the computer <a href="/wiki/Software_development" title="Software development">software development</a> process and related business management functions. It is one of the joint <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO) and <a href="/wiki/International_Electrotechnical_Commission" title="International Electrotechnical Commission">International Electrotechnical Commission</a> (IEC) standards, which was developed by the ISO and IEC joint subcommittee, <a href="/wiki/ISO/IEC_JTC_1/SC_7" title="ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 7">ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 7</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>ISO/IEC 15504 was initially derived from process lifecycle standard <a href="/wiki/ISO/IEC_12207" title="ISO/IEC 12207">ISO/IEC 12207</a> and from maturity models like Bootstrap, <a href="/wiki/Trillium_Model" title="Trillium Model">Trillium</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Capability_Maturity_Model" title="Capability Maturity Model">Capability Maturity Model</a> (CMM).</p>
<p>ISO/IEC 15504 has been revised by: <i><a href="/wiki/ISO/IEC_33001" title="ISO/IEC 33001">ISO/IEC 33001</a>:2015 Information technology – Process assessment – Concepts and terminology</i> as of March, 2015 and is no longer available at ISO.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ISO/IEC 15504 is the reference model for the maturity models (consisting of capability levels which in turn consist of the process attributes and further consist of generic practices) against which the assessors can place the evidence that they collect during their assessment, so that the assessors can give an overall determination of the organization's capabilities for delivering products (software, systems, and IT services).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISO.2FIEC_15504-2_Clause_5_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISO.2FIEC_15504-2_Clause_5-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A working group was formed in 1993 to draft the international standard and used the acronym SPICE. SPICE initially stood for <i><a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">Software</a> Process Improvement and Capability Evaluation</i>, but in consideration of French concerns over the meaning of <i>evaluation</i>, SPICE has now been renamed <i>Software Process Improvement and Capability Determination</i>. SPICE is still used for the user group of the standard, and the title for the annual conference. The first SPICE was held in <a href="/wiki/Limerick" title="Limerick">Limerick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> in 2000, <i>SPICE 2003</i> was hosted by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ESA" title="ESA">ESA</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <i>SPICE 2004</i> was hosted in <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <i>SPICE 2005</i> in <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <i>SPICE 2006</i> in <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, <i>SPICE 2007</i> in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, <i>SPICE 2008</i> in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and <i>SPICE 2009</i> in <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>.</p>
<p>The first versions of the standard focused exclusively on <a href="/wiki/Software_development" title="Software development">software development</a> processes. This was expanded to cover all related processes in a software business, for example <a href="/wiki/Project_management" title="Project management">project management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Configuration_management" title="Configuration management">configuration management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quality_assurance" title="Quality assurance">quality assurance</a>, and so on. The list of processes covered grew to cover six business areas: organizational, management, engineering, acquisition supply, support, and operations.</p>
<p>In a major revision to the draft standard in 2004, the process reference model was removed and is now related to the <a href="/wiki/ISO/IEC_12207" title="ISO/IEC 12207">ISO/IEC 12207</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Software_Lifecycle_Processes" title="Software Lifecycle Processes">Software Lifecycle Processes</a>). The issued standard now specifies the measurement framework and can use different process reference models. There are five general and industry models in use.</p>
<p>Part 5 specifies software process assessment and part 6 specifies system process assessment.</p>
<p>The latest work in the ISO standards working group includes creation of a maturity model, which is planned to become ISO/IEC 15504 part 7.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_standard">The standard</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The standard">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="metadata plainlinks ambox ambox-content ambox-Update" role="presentation">
<tr>
<td class="mbox-image">

<td class="mbox-text"><span class="mbox-text-span">This section's <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute">factual accuracy</a> may be compromised due to out-of-date information</b>. <span class="hide-when-compact">Please update this article to reflect recent events or newly available information.</span> <small><i>(October 2011)</i></small></span></td>

<p>The Technical Report (TR) document for ISO/IEC TR 15504 was divided into 9 parts. The initial International Standard was recreated in 5 parts. This was proposed from Japan when the TRs were published at 1997.</p>
<p>The International Standard (IS) version of ISO/IEC 15504 now comprises 6 parts. The 7th part is currently in an advanced Final Draft Standard form<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and work has started on part 8.</p>
<p><i>Part 1</i> of ISO/IEC TR 15504 explains the concepts and gives an overview of the framework.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reference_model">Reference model</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reference model">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>ISO/IEC 15504 contains a <i><a href="/wiki/Reference_model" title="Reference model">reference model</a></i>. The reference model defines a <i>process dimension</i> and a <i>capability dimension</i>.</p>
<p>The process dimension in the reference model is not the subject of <i>part 2</i> of ISO/IEC 15504, but part 2 refers to external process lifecycle standards including ISO/IEC 12207 and ISO/IEC 15288.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The standard defines means to verify conformity of reference models.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Processes">Processes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Processes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <i>process dimension</i> defines processes divided into the five process categories of:</p>
<ul>
<li>customer-supplier</li>
<li>engineering</li>
<li>supporting</li>
<li>management</li>
<li>organization</li>
</ul>
<p>With new parts being published, the process categories will expand, particularly for IT service process categories and enterprise process categories.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Capability_levels_and_process_attributes">Capability levels and process attributes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Capability levels and process attributes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>For each process, ISO/IEC 15504 defines a <i>capability level</i> on the following scale:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISO.2FIEC_15504-2_Clause_5_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ISO.2FIEC_15504-2_Clause_5-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<tr>
<th>Level</th>
<th>Name</th>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>5</td>
<td>Optimizing process</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>4</td>
<td>Predictable process</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>3</td>
<td>Established process</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>2</td>
<td>Managed process</td>
</tr>
<tr>

</tr>
<tr>

<td>Incomplete process</td>

</table>
<p>The capability of processes is measured using process attributes. The international standard defines nine process attributes:</p>
<ul>
<li>1.1 Process performance</li>
<li>2.1 Performance management</li>
<li>2.2 Work product management</li>
<li>3.1 Process definition</li>
<li>3.2 Process deployment</li>
<li>4.1 Process measurement</li>
<li>4.2 Process control</li>
<li>5.1 Process innovation</li>
<li>5.2 Process <a href="/wiki/Operations_research" title="Operations research">optimization</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Each process attribute consists of one or more generic practices, which are further elaborated into practice indicators to aid assessment performance.</p>
<p>Each process attribute is assessed on a four-point (N-P-L-F) rating scale:</p>
<ul>
<li>Not achieved (0 - 15%)</li>
<li>Partially achieved (&gt;15% - 50%)</li>
<li>Largely achieved (&gt;50%- 85%)</li>
<li>Fully achieved (&gt;85% - 100%).</li>
</ul>
<p>The rating is based upon evidence collected against the practice indicators, which demonstrate fulfillment of the process attribute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Assessments">Assessments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Assessments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>ISO/IEC 15504 provides a guide for <i>performing an assessment</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>This includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>the assessment process</li>
<li>the model for the assessment</li>
<li>any tools used in the assessment</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Assessment_process">Assessment process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Assessment process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Performing assessments is the subject of <i>parts 2 and 3</i> of ISO/IEC 15504.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Part 2 is the normative part and part 3 gives a guidance to fulfill the requirements in part 2.</p>
<p>One of the requirements is to use a conformant assessment method for the assessment process. The actual method is not specified in the standard although the standard places requirements on the method, method developers and assessors using the method.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2007a_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-2007a-9">[9]</a></sup> The standard provides general guidance to assessors and this must be supplemented by undergoing formal training and detailed guidance during initial assessments.</p>
<p>The assessment process can be generalized as the following steps:</p>
<ul>
<li>initiate an assessment (assessment sponsor)</li>
<li>select assessor and assessment team</li>
<li>plan the assessment, including processes and organizational unit to be assessed (lead assessor and assessment team)</li>
<li>pre-assessment briefing</li>
<li>data collection</li>
<li>data validation</li>
<li>process rating</li>
<li>reporting the assessment result</li>
</ul>
<p>An assessor can collect data on a process by various means, including interviews with persons performing the process, collecting documents and quality records, and collecting statistical process data. The assessor validates this data to ensure it is accurate and completely covers the assessment scope. The assessor assesses this data (using their expert judgment) against a process's base practices and the capability dimension's generic practices in the process rating step. Process rating requires some exercising of expert judgment on the part of the assessor and this is the reason that there are requirements on assessor qualifications and competency. The process rating is then presented as a preliminary finding to the sponsor (and preferably also to the persons assessed) to ensure that they agree that the assessment is accurate. In a few cases, there may be feedback requiring further assessment before a final process rating is made.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2007b_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-2007b-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Assessment_model">Assessment model</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Assessment model">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <i>process assessment model (PAM)</i> is the detailed model used for an actual assessment. This is an elaboration of the process reference model (PRM) provided by the process lifecycle standards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The process assessment model (PAM) in part 5 is based on the process reference model (PRM) for software: ISO/IEC 12207.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The process assessment model in part 6 is based on the process reference model for systems: ISO/IEC 15288.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The standard allows other models to be used instead, if they meet ISO/IEC 15504's criteria, which include a defined community of interest and meeting the requirements for content (i.e. process purpose, process outcomes and assessment indicators).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tools_used_in_the_assessment">Tools used in the assessment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Tools used in the assessment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>There exist several assessment tools. The simplest comprise paper-based tools. In general, they are laid out to incorporate the assessment model indicators, including the base practice indicators and generic practice indicators. Assessors write down the assessment results and notes supporting the assessment judgment.</p>
<p>There are a limited number of computer based tools that present the indicators and allow users to enter the assessment judgment and notes in formatted screens, as well as automate the collated assessment result (i.e. the process attribute ratings) and creating reports.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Assessor_qualifications_and_competency">Assessor qualifications and competency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Assessor qualifications and competency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For a successful assessment, the <i>assessor</i> must have a suitable level of the relevant skills and experience.</p>
<p>These skills include:</p>
<ul>
<li>personal qualities such as <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communication skills</a>.</li>
<li>relevant education and training and experience.</li>
<li>specific skills for particular categories, e.g. management skills for the management category.</li>
<li>ISO/IEC 15504 related training and experience in process capability assessments.</li>
</ul>
<p>The competency of assessors is the subject of <i>part 3</i> of ISO/IEC 15504.</p>
<p>In summary, the ISO/IEC 15504 specific training and experience for assessors comprise:</p>
<ul>
<li>completion of a 5-day lead assessor training course</li>
<li>performing at least one assessment successfully under supervision of a competent lead assessor</li>
<li>performing at least one assessment successfully as a lead assessor under the supervision of a competent lead assessor. The competent lead assessor defines when the assessment is successfully performed. There exist schemes for certifying assessors and guiding lead assessors in making this judgement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2007a_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-2007a-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>ISO/IEC 15504 can be used in two <i>contexts</i>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Process improvement, and</li>
<li>Capability determination (= evaluation of supplier's process capability).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Process_improvement">Process improvement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Process improvement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>ISO/IEC 15504 can be used to perform process improvement within a technology organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Process improvement is always difficult, and initiatives often fail, so it is important to understand the initial baseline level (process capability level), and to assess the situation after an improvement project. ISO 15504 provides a standard for assessing the organization's capacity to deliver at each of these stages.</p>
<p>In particular, the reference framework of ISO/IEC 15504 provides a structure for defining objectives, which facilitates specific programs to achieve these objectives.</p>
<p>Process improvement is the subject of <i>part 4</i> of ISO/IEC 15504. It specifies requirements for improvement programmes and provides guidance on planning and executing improvements, including a description of an eight step improvement programme. Following this improvement programme is not mandatory and several alternative improvement programmes exist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2007b_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-2007b-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Capability_determination">Capability determination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Capability determination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>An organization considering <a href="/wiki/Outsourcing" title="Outsourcing">outsourcing</a> software development needs to have a good understanding of the capability of potential suppliers to deliver.</p>
<p>ISO/IEC 15504 (Part 4) can also be used to inform supplier selection decisions. The ISO/IEC 15504 framework provides a framework for assessing proposed suppliers, as assessed either by the organization itself, or by an independent assessor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The organization can determine a <i>target capability</i> for suppliers, based on the organization's needs, and then assess suppliers against a set of target process profiles that specify this target capability. Part 4 of the ISO/IEC 15504 specifies the high level requirements and an initiative has been started to create an extended part of the standard covering target process profiles. Target process profiles are particularly important in contexts where the organization (for example, a government department) is required to accept the cheapest <i>qualifying</i> vendor. This also enables suppliers to identify gaps between their current capability and the level required by a potential customer, and to undertake improvement to achieve the contract requirements (i.e. become qualified). Work on extending the value of capability determination includes a method called Practical Process Profiles - which uses risk as the determining factor in setting target process profiles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2007b_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-2007b-10">[10]</a></sup> Combining risk and processes promotes improvement with active risk reduction, hence reducing the likelihood of problems occurring.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acceptance_of_ISO.2FIEC_15504">Acceptance of ISO/IEC 15504</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_15504&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Acceptance of ISO/IEC 15504">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ISO/IEC 15504 has been successful as:</p>
<ul>
<li>ISO/IEC 15504 is publicly available through National Standards Bodies.</li>
<li>It has the support of the international community.</li>
<li>Over 4000 assessments have been performed to date.</li>
<li>Major sectors are leading the pace such as automotive, space and medical systems with industry relevant variants.</li>
<li>Domain-specific models like <i>Automotive SPICE</i> and <i>SPICE 4 SPACE</i> can be derived from it.</li>
<li>There have been many international initiatives to support take-up such as <a href="/wiki/ISO_29110" title="ISO 29110">SPiCE for small and very small entities</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>On the other hand, ISO/IEC 15504 has not yet been as successful as the <a href="/wiki/Capability_Maturity_Model_Integration" title="Capability Maturity Model Integration">CMMI</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. This has been for several reasons:</p>
<ul>
<li>ISO/IEC 15504 is not available as free download but must be purchased from the ISO (Automotive SPICE on the other hand can be freely downloaded from the link supplied below.) CMM and CMMI are available as free downloads from the SEI website.</li>
<li>The CMMI is actively sponsored (by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Department_of_Defense" title="US Department of Defense">US Department of Defense</a>).</li>
<li>The CMM was created first, and reached critical 'market' share before ISO 15504 became available.</li>
<li>The CMM has subsequently been replaced by the CMMI, which incorporates many of the ideas of ISO/IEC 15504, but also retains the benefits of the CMM.</li>
</ul>
<p>Like the CMM, ISO/IEC 15504 was created in a development context, making it difficult to apply in a service management context. But work has started to develop an <a href="/wiki/ISO/IEC_20000" title="ISO/IEC 20000">ISO/IEC 20000</a>-based process reference model (ISO/IEC 20000-4) that can serve as a basis for a process assessment model. This is planned to become part 8 to the standard (ISO/IEC 15504-8). In addition there are methods available that adapt its use to various contexts.</p>
