<p>A <b>software engineer</b> is a person who applies the principles of <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a> to the design, development, maintenance, testing, and evaluation of the <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a> and systems that make computers or anything containing software work.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to the mid-1960s, software practitioners called themselves <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_programmer" title="Computer programmer">computer programmers</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Software_developer" title="Software developer">software developers</a>,</i> regardless of their actual jobs. Many people prefer to call themselves <i>software developer</i> and <i>programmer</i>, because most widely agree what these terms mean, while <i>software engineer</i> is still being debated. In many companies, the titles <i>programmer</i> and <i>software developer</i> were changed to <i>software engineer</i>, for many categories of programmers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>In May 2015, the United States <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Labor_Statistics" title="Bureau of Labor Statistics">U. S. Bureau of Labor Statistics</a> published an updated count of software engineers which has gone up from 760,840 in 2004 to 1,554,960 in 2015; in the same period there were some 1,610,480 practitioners employed in the U.S. in all other engineering disciplines combined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The label software engineer is used very liberally in the corporate world. Very few of the practicing software engineers actually hold Engineering degrees from accredited universities.</p>

<p>About half of all practitioners today have <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">degrees</a> in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Information_systems" title="Information systems">information systems</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a>. A small, but growing, number of practitioners have software engineering degrees. In 1987, <a href="/wiki/Imperial_College_London" title="Imperial College London">Imperial College London</a> introduced the first three-year software engineering <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's degree</a> in the UK and the world; in the following year, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Sheffield" title="University of Sheffield">University of Sheffield</a> established a similar program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1996, the <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Institute_of_Technology" title="Rochester Institute of Technology">Rochester Institute of Technology</a> established the first software engineering bachelor's degree program in the United States, however, it did not obtain <a href="/wiki/ABET" title="ABET">ABET</a> accreditation until 2003, the same time as <a href="/wiki/Rice_University" title="Rice University">Rice University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clarkson_University" title="Clarkson University">Clarkson University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_School_of_Engineering" title="Milwaukee School of Engineering">Milwaukee School of Engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_State_University" title="Mississippi State University">Mississippi State University</a> obtained theirs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABETACCRED1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABETACCRED1-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1997, PSG College of Technology in Coimbatore, India was the first to start a five-year integrated Master of Science degree in Software Engineering.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Since then, software engineering undergraduate degrees have been established at many universities. A standard international curriculum for undergraduate software engineering degrees was recently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (August 2011)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> defined by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CCSE" title="CCSE">CCSE</a>. As of 2004<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, in the U.S., about 50 universities offer software engineering degrees, which teach both computer science and engineering principles and practices. The first software engineering <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">Master's degree</a> was established at <a href="/wiki/Seattle_University" title="Seattle University">Seattle University</a> in 1979. Since then graduate software engineering degrees have been made available from many more universities. Likewise in Canada, the Canadian Engineering Accreditation Board (CEAB) of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Council_of_Professional_Engineers" title="Canadian Council of Professional Engineers">Canadian Council of Professional Engineers</a> has recognized several software engineering programs.</p>
<p>In 1998, the US <a href="/wiki/Naval_Postgraduate_School" title="Naval Postgraduate School">Naval Postgraduate School</a> (NPS) established the first <a href="/wiki/Doctorate" title="Doctorate">doctorate</a> program in Software Engineering in the world.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Additionally, many online advanced degrees in Software Engineering have appeared such as the Master of Science in Software Engineering (MSE) degree offered through the Computer Science and Engineering Department at <a href="/wiki/California_State_University,_Fullerton" title="California State University, Fullerton">California State University, Fullerton</a>. Steve McConnell opines that because most universities teach computer science rather than software engineering, there is a shortage of true software engineers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> ETS University and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UQAM" title="UQAM">UQAM</a> (Université du Québec à Montréal) were mandated by IEEE to develop the Software Engineering Body of Knowledge (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SWEBOK" title="SWEBOK">SWEBOK</a>), which has become an ISO standard describing the body of knowledge covered by a software engineer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISO_19759_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISO_19759-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_degrees">Other degrees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other degrees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In business, some software engineering practitioners have <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Management_Information_Systems" title="Management Information Systems">MIS</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_information_systems" title="Computer information systems">computer information systems</a> degrees. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Embedded_systems" title="Embedded systems">embedded systems</a>, some have <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronics_engineering" title="Electronics engineering">electronics engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> with emphasis in "embedded systems" or <a href="/wiki/Computer_engineering" title="Computer engineering">computer engineering</a> degrees, because embedded software often requires a detailed understanding of hardware. In <a href="/wiki/Medical_software" title="Medical software">medical software</a>, practitioners may have <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical_informatics" title="Medical informatics">medical informatics</a>, general medical, or biology degrees.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Some practitioners have <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a> degrees. Some have <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a> (logic in particular) or other non-technical degrees.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>For instance, <a href="/wiki/Barry_Boehm" title="Barry Boehm">Barry Boehm</a> earned degrees in mathematics. And, others have no degrees.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Profession">Profession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Profession">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Employment">Employment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Employment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering_demographics" title="Software engineering demographics">Software engineering demographics</a></div>
<p>Most software engineers work as employees or contractors. Software engineers work with businesses, government agencies (civilian or military), and non-profit organizations. Some software engineers work on their own as consulting software engineers. Some organizations have specialists to perform each of the tasks in the <a href="/wiki/Software_development_process" title="Software development process">software development process</a>. Other organizations required software engineers to do many or all of them. Entry-level software engineer or associate software engineer may be best. Some companies offer software engineer as an entry-level position. In large projects, people may specialize in only one role. In small projects, people may fill several or all roles at the same time. Specializations include: in industry (<a href="/wiki/Requirements_analysis" title="Requirements analysis">analysts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Software_architecture" title="Software architecture">architects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Software_developer" title="Software developer">developers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Software_testing" title="Software testing">testers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Technical_support" title="Technical support">technical support</a>, <a href="/wiki/Project_management" title="Project management">managers</a>) and in academia (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Educator" title="Educator">educators</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Researcher" title="Researcher">researchers</a>).</p>
<p>There is considerable debate over the future employment prospects for Software Engineers and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Information_Technology" title="Information Technology">IT</a> Professionals. For example, an online futures market called the <a class="external text" href="http://www.ideosphere.com/fx-bin/Claim?claim=ITJOBS" rel="nofollow">Future of IT Jobs in America</a> attempts to answer whether there will be more IT jobs, including software engineers, in 2012 than there were in 2002. Possible opportunities for Advancement can be as a Software Engineer, then to a Senior Software Engineer, or straight to a Senior Software Engineer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> depending on skills and reputation. Services exist that are trying to better gauge the coding ability of an engineer, given not all engineers progress their abilities at the same rate, and to make it easier for both employers and employees to find a good match in terms of jobs.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This job is office-based, and most of the work is done during normal office hours, but can sometimes lead to working away and working late or during weekends, depending on where and when the client is situated. The job can also be done at home or anywhere a computer is set up. Some high-profile companies have encouraged software engineers to work for long hours; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apple_Inc" title="Apple Inc">Apple</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Steve_Jobs" title="Steve Jobs">Steve Jobs</a> set up a culture where engineers would never take holidays and work throughout weekends, yet love what they were doing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_of_globalization">Impact of globalization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Impact of globalization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most students in the developed world have avoided degrees related to software engineering because of the fear of <a href="/wiki/Offshore_outsourcing" title="Offshore outsourcing">offshore outsourcing</a> (importing software products or services from other countries) and of being displaced by <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Worker_Visa" title="Foreign Worker Visa">foreign visa workers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Although government statistics do not currently show a threat to software engineering itself; a related career, <a href="/wiki/Computer_programming" title="Computer programming">computer programming</a> does appear to have been affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Often one is expected to start out as a computer programmer before being promoted to software engineer. Thus, the career path to software engineering may be rough, especially during recessions.</p>
<p>Some career counselors suggest a student also focus on "people skills" and business skills rather than purely technical skills because such "soft skills" are allegedly more difficult to offshore. Reasonable command over reading, writing &amp; speaking English is asked by most of employers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It is the quasi-management aspects of software engineering that appear to be what has kept it from being impacted by globalization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prizes">Prizes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Prizes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are several prizes in the field of software engineering:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Codie_awards" title="Codie awards">CODiE awards</a> is a yearly award issued by the Software and Information Industry Association for excellence in software development within the software industry.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jolt_Awards" title="Jolt Awards">Jolt Awards</a> are awards in the software industry.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stevens_Award" title="Stevens Award">Stevens Award</a> is a software engineering award given in memory of Wayne Stevens.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_of_the_title_.22Engineer.22">Use of the title "Engineer"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title='Edit section: Use of the title "Engineer"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Suitability_of_the_term">Suitability of the term</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Suitability of the term">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Regulation_and_licensure_in_engineering" title="Regulation and licensure in engineering">Regulation and licensure in engineering</a></div>
<p>Many people<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (March 2014)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> believe that <i>software engineering</i> implies a certain level of academic training, professional discipline, adherence to formal processes, and especially legal liability that often are not applied in cases of software development. A common analogy is that working in <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a> does not make one a <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineer" title="Civil engineer">civil engineer</a>, and so writing <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">code</a> does not make one a software engineer. Furthermore, because computing doesn't utilize the methods of mathematical physics common to all conventional engineering disciplines, it's more appropriate to call those engaged in this occupation as software developers, computer scientists or similar.</p>
<p>In 1978, computer scientist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edsger_Wybe_Dijkstra" title="Edsger Wybe Dijkstra">E. W. Dijkstra</a> wrote in a paper that the coining of the term <i>software engineer</i> was not useful since it was an inappropriate analogy, "The existence of the mere term has been the base of a number of extremely shallow—and false—analogies, which just confuse the issue...Computers are such exceptional gadgets that there is good reason to assume that most analogies with other disciplines are too shallow to be of any positive value, are even so shallow that they are only confusing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In each of the last few decades, at least one radical new approach has entered the mainstream of software development (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Structured_programming" title="Structured programming">Structured Programming</a>, <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_programming" title="Object-oriented programming">Object Orientation</a>), implying that the field is still changing too rapidly to be considered an engineering discipline. Proponents argue that the supposedly radical new approaches are evolutionary rather than revolutionary.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Individual commentators have disagreed sharply on how to define <i>software engineering</i> or its legitimacy as an engineering discipline. <a href="/wiki/David_Parnas" title="David Parnas">David Parnas</a> has said that software engineering is, in fact, a form of engineering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Steve_McConnell" title="Steve McConnell">Steve McConnell</a> has said that it is not, but that it should be.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Donald_Knuth" title="Donald Knuth">Donald Knuth</a> has said that programming is an art and a science.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Edsger_W._Dijkstra" title="Edsger W. Dijkstra">Edsger W. Dijkstra</a> claimed that the terms <i>software engineering</i> and <i>software engineer</i> have been misused<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Synthesis_of_published_material" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag may be based upon an improper synthesis of sources. (April 2015)">improper synthesis?</span></a></i>]</sup> and should be considered harmful, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulatory_classification">Regulatory classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Regulatory classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Iceland">Iceland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Iceland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The use of the title <i>tölvunarfræðingur</i> (e. <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a>) is protected by law in Iceland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Software engineering is taught in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_Science" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a> departments in Icelandic universities. Icelandic law state that a permission must be obtained from the Minister of Industry when the degree was awarded abroad, prior to use of the title. The title is only awarded to those who have obtained a master's degree in Software Engineering from a recognized higher educational institution.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Disputed statement"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (August 2014)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:Software_engineer#t.C3.B6lvunarfr.C3.A6.C3.B0ingur_does_not_require_MS_only_BS_at_least_for_domestic_degrees.2C_I_think_also_foreign.." title="Talk:Software engineer">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The U.K. has seen the alignment of the Information Technology Professional and the Engineering Professionals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.27British_Computer_Society.27_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-.27British_Computer_Society.27-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag needs to be fact-checked with the cited source(s). (March 2014)">verification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In New Zealand, <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Professional_Engineers_New_Zealand" title="Institution of Professional Engineers New Zealand">IPENZ</a>, the professional engineering organization entrusted by the New Zealand government with legal power to license and regulate chartered engineers (CPEng), recognizes software engineering as a legitimate branch of professional engineering and accepts application of software engineers to obtain chartered status provided he or she has a tertiary degree of approved subjects. Software Engineering is included but Computer Science is normally not.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Canada the use of the job title "Engineer" is controlled in each province by self-regulating professional engineering organizations who are also tasked with enforcement of the governing legislation. The intent is that any individual holding themselves out as an engineer has been verified to have been educated to a certain accredited level and their professional practice is subject to a code of ethics and peer scrutiny. It is also illegal to use the title Engineer in Canada unless an individual is licensed.</p>
<p>IT professionals with degrees in other fields (such as computer science or information systems) are restricted from using the title "Software Engineer", or wording "Software Engineer" in a title, depending on their province or territory of residence. In some instances, cases have been taken to court regarding the illegal use of the protected title "Software Engineer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.27Professional_Engineers_of_Ontario.27_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-.27Professional_Engineers_of_Ontario.27-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Software_engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Labor_Statistics" title="Bureau of Labor Statistics">U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics</a> classifies <i>computer software engineers</i> as a subcategory of "computer specialists", along with occupations such as computer scientist, programmer, and network administrator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bls.gov_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-bls.gov-24">[24]</a></sup> The BLS classifies all other engineering disciplines, including computer hardware engineers, as "engineers".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some of the states regulate the use of terms such as "computer engineer" and even "software engineer". These states include at least Texas<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and Florida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leg.state.fl.us_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-leg.state.fl.us-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>There is also a new PE (Professional Engineer) exam beginning in April 2013 for Software Engineering specifically as the process of tougher regulation moves forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
