<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Architectural pattern</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/w/index.php?title=Architectural_pattern_(computer_science)&amp;redirect=no" title="Architectural pattern (computer science)">Architectural pattern (computer science)</a>)</span></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="hatnote" role="note">For the use of the word "pattern" in the field of architecture, see <a href="/wiki/Pattern_(architecture)" title="Pattern (architecture)">Pattern (architecture)</a>.</div>
<p>An <b>architectural pattern</b> is a general, reusable solution to a commonly occurring problem in <a href="/wiki/Software_architecture" title="Software architecture">software architecture</a> within a given context.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TMD_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TMD-1">[1]</a></sup> Architectural patterns are similar to <a href="/wiki/Software_design_pattern" title="Software design pattern">software design pattern</a> but have a broader scope. The architectural patterns address various issues in <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Computer_hardware" title="Computer hardware">computer hardware</a> performance limitations, <a href="/wiki/High_availability" title="High availability">high availability</a> and minimization of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Business_risk" title="Business risk">business risk</a>. Some architectural patterns have been implemented within <a href="/wiki/Software_framework" title="Software framework">software frameworks</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Architectural_pattern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Even though an architectural pattern conveys an image of a system, it is not an architecture. An architectural pattern is a concept that solves and delineates some essential cohesive elements of a software architecture. Countless different architectures may implement the same pattern and share the related characteristics. Patterns are often defined as "strictly described and commonly available".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> When it is strictly described and commonly available, it is a pattern.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architectural_style">Architectural style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Architectural_pattern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architectural style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Following traditional building architecture, a 'software architectural style' is a specific method of construction, characterized by the features that make it notable" (<a href="/wiki/Architectural_style" title="Architectural style">Architectural style</a>). "An architectural style defines: a family of systems in terms of a pattern of structural organization; a vocabulary of components and connectors, with constraints on how they can be combined."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SG_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-SG-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>"An architectural style is a named collection of architectural design decisions that (1) are applicable in a given development context, (2) constrain architectural design decisions that are specific to a particular system within that context, and (3) elicit beneficial qualities in each resulting system."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TMD_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TMD-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some treat architectural patterns and architectural styles as the same,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSDN_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-MSDN-5">[5]</a></sup> some treat styles as specializations of patterns. What they have in common is both patterns and styles are idioms for architects to use, they "provide a common language"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSDN_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-MSDN-5">[5]</a></sup> or "vocabulary"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SG_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-SG-4">[4]</a></sup> with which to describe classes of systems.</p>
<p>The main difference is that a pattern can be seen as a solution to a problem, while a style is more general and does not require a problem to solve for its appearance.</p>
