<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Computer engineering</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p><b>Computer engineering</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Discipline_(academia)" title="Discipline (academia)">discipline</a> that integrates several fields of <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> required to develop <a href="/wiki/Computer_hardware" title="Computer hardware">computer hardware</a> and software.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Computer engineers usually have training in <a href="/wiki/Electronic_engineering" title="Electronic engineering">electronic engineering</a> (or <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a>), <a href="/wiki/Software_design" title="Software design">software design</a>, and hardware-software integration instead of only <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a> or electronic engineering. Computer engineers are involved in many hardware and software aspects of computing, from the design of individual <a href="/wiki/Microcontroller" title="Microcontroller">microcontrollers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microprocessor" title="Microprocessor">microprocessors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">personal computers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Supercomputer" title="Supercomputer">supercomputers</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Circuit_design" title="Circuit design">circuit design</a>. This field of engineering not only focuses on how computer systems themselves work, but also how they integrate into the larger picture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Usual tasks involving computer engineers include writing software and <a href="/wiki/Firmware" title="Firmware">firmware</a> for <a href="/wiki/Embedded_system" title="Embedded system">embedded</a> <a href="/wiki/Microcontroller" title="Microcontroller">microcontrollers</a>, designing <a href="/wiki/Very-large-scale_integration" title="Very-large-scale integration">VLSI</a> chips, designing <a href="/wiki/Analog_device" title="Analog device">analog</a> <a href="/wiki/Sensor" title="Sensor">sensors</a>, designing mixed signal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Circuit_board" title="Circuit board">circuit boards</a>, and designing <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating systems</a>. Computer engineers are also suited for <a href="/wiki/Robotics" title="Robotics">robotics</a> research, which relies heavily on using digital systems to control and monitor electrical systems like <a href="/wiki/Electric_motor" title="Electric motor">motors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer-mediated_communication" title="Computer-mediated communication">communications</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sensor" title="Sensor">sensors</a>.</p>
<p>In many institutions, computer engineering students are allowed to choose areas of in-depth study in their junior and senior year, because the full breadth of knowledge used in the design and application of computers is beyond the scope of an undergraduate degree. Other institutions may require engineering students to complete one or two years of General Engineering before declaring computer engineering as their primary focus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first computer engineering degree program in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> was established at <a href="/wiki/Case_Western_Reserve_University" title="Case Western Reserve University">Case Western Reserve University</a> in 1972. As of 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, there were 238 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Accreditation_Board_for_Engineering_and_Technology" title="Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology">ABET</a>-accredited computer engineering programs in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In Europe, accreditation of computer engineering schools is done by a variety of agencies part of the <a href="/wiki/EQANIE" title="EQANIE">EQANIE</a> network. Due to increasing job requirements for engineers who can concurrently design hardware, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_software" title="Computer software">software</a>, firmware, and manage all forms of computer systems used in industry, some tertiary institutions around the world offer a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a> generally called computer engineering. Both computer engineering and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_engineering" title="Electronic engineering">electronic engineering</a> programs include analog and digital circuit design in their curriculum. As with most <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> disciplines, having a sound knowledge of <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> and science is necessary for computer engineers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two major specialties in computer engineering: software and hardware.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_software_engineering">Computer software engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Computer software engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">Software engineering</a></div>
<p>Computer software engineers develop, design, and test software. Some software engineers design, construct, and maintain computer programs for companies. Some set up networks such as "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intranets" title="Intranets">intranets</a>" for companies. Others make or install new software or upgrade computer systems. Computer software engineers can also work in application design. This involves designing or coding new programs and applications to meet the needs of a business or individual. Computer software engineers can also work as freelancers and sell their software products/applications to an enterprise/individual.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-C.S.E._8-0"><a href="#cite_note-C.S.E.-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_hardware_engineering">Computer hardware engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Computer hardware engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most computer hardware engineers research, develop, design, and test various computer equipment. This can range from circuit boards and microprocessors to routers. Some update existing computer equipment to be more efficient and work with newer software. Most computer hardware engineers work in research laboratories and high-tech manufacturing firms. Some also work for the federal government. According to <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Labor_Statistics" title="Bureau of Labor Statistics">BLS</a>, 95% of computer hardware engineers work in metropolitan areas.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> They generally work full-time. Approximately 33% of their work requires more than 40 hours a week. The median salary for employed qualified computer hardware engineers (2012) was $100,920 per year or $48.52 per hour. Computer hardware engineers held 83,300 jobs in 2012 in the USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHE_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-CHE-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specialty_areas">Specialty areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Specialty areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are many specialty areas in the field of computer engineering.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coding.2C_cryptography.2C_and_information_protection">Coding, cryptography, and information protection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Coding, cryptography, and information protection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Information_security" title="Information security">Information security</a></div>
<p>Computer engineers work in coding, cryptography, and information protection to develop new methods for protecting various information, such as digital images and music, fragmentation, copyright infringement and other forms of tampering. Examples include work on wireless communications, multi-antenna systems, optical transmission, and <a href="/wiki/Digital_watermarking" title="Digital watermarking">digital watermarking</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCCC_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-SCCC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Communications_and_wireless_networks">Communications and wireless networks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Communications and wireless networks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communications_networks" title="Communications networks">Communications networks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wireless_network" title="Wireless network">Wireless network</a></div>
<p>Those focusing on communications and wireless networks, work advancements in telecommunications systems and networks (especially wireless networks), modulation and error-control coding, and information theory. High-speed network design, interference suppression and modulation, design and analysis of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fault-tolerant_system" title="Fault-tolerant system">fault-tolerant system</a>, and storage and transmission schemes are all a part of this specialty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCCC_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-SCCC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Compilers_and_operating_systems">Compilers and operating systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Compilers and operating systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Compiler" title="Compiler">Compiler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">Operating system</a></div>
<p>This specialty focuses on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compilers" title="Compilers">compilers</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operating_systems" title="Operating systems">operating systems</a> design and development. Engineers in this field develop new operating system architecture, program analysis techniques, and new techniques to assure quality. Examples of work in this field includes post-link-time code transformation algorithm development and new operating system development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCCC_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-SCCC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Computational_science_and_engineering">Computational science and engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Computational science and engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computational_science_and_engineering" title="Computational science and engineering">Computational science and engineering</a></div>
<p>Computational Science and Engineering is a relatively new discipline. According to the Sloan Career Cornerstone Center, individuals working in this area, "computational methods are applied to formulate and solve complex mathematical problems in engineering and the physical and the social sciences. Examples include aircraft design, the plasma processing of nanometer features on semiconductor wafers, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VLSI" title="VLSI">VLSI</a> circuit design, radar detection systems, ion transport through biological channels, and much more".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCCC_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-SCCC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_networks.2C_mobile_computing.2C_and_distributed_systems">Computer networks, mobile computing, and distributed systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Computer networks, mobile computing, and distributed systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Computer_network" title="Computer network">Computer network</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mobile_computing" title="Mobile computing">Mobile computing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Distributed_computing" title="Distributed computing">Distributed computing</a></div>
<p>In this specialty, engineers build integrated environments for computing, communications, and information access. Examples include shared-channel wireless networks, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adaptive_Management" title="Adaptive Management">adaptive resource management</a> in various systems, and improving the quality of service in mobile and ATM environments. Some other examples include work on wireless network systems and fast Ethernet cluster wired systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCCC_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-SCCC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_systems:_architecture.2C_parallel_processing.2C_and_dependability">Computer systems: architecture, parallel processing, and dependability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Computer systems: architecture, parallel processing, and dependability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Computer_architecture" title="Computer architecture">Computer architecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parallel_computing" title="Parallel computing">Parallel computing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dependability" title="Dependability">Dependability</a></div>
<p>Engineers working in computer systems work on research projects that allow for reliable, secure, and high-performance computer systems. Projects such as designing processors for multi-threading and parallel processing are included in this field. Other examples of work in this field include development of new theories, algorithms, and other tools that add performance to computer systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCCC_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-SCCC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_vision_and_robotics">Computer vision and robotics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Computer vision and robotics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Computer_vision" title="Computer vision">Computer vision</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robotics" title="Robotics">Robotics</a></div>
<p>In this specialty, computer engineers focus on developing <a href="/wiki/Visual_sensor_network" title="Visual sensor network">visual sensing technology</a> to sense an environment, representation of an environment, and manipulation of the environment. The gathered three-dimensional information is then implemented to perform a variety of tasks. These include, improved human modeling, image communication, and human-computer interfaces, as well as devices such as special-purpose cameras with versatile vision sensors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCCC_10-6"><a href="#cite_note-SCCC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Embedded_systems">Embedded systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Embedded systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Embedded_systems" title="Embedded systems">Embedded systems</a></div>
<p>Individuals working in this area design technology for enhancing the speed, reliability, and performance of systems. Embedded systems are found in many devices from a small FM radio to the space shuttle. According to the Sloan Cornerstone Career Center, ongoing developments in embedded systems include "automated vehicles and equipment to conduct search and rescue, automated transportation systems, and human-robot coordination to repair equipment in space."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCCC_10-7"><a href="#cite_note-SCCC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Integrated_circuits.2C_VLSI_design.2C_testing_and_CAD">Integrated circuits, VLSI design, testing and CAD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Integrated circuits, VLSI design, testing and CAD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit" title="Integrated circuit">Integrated circuit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Very-large-scale_integration" title="Very-large-scale integration">Very-large-scale integration</a></div>
<p>This specialty of computer engineering requires adequate knowledge of electronics and electrical systems. Engineers working in this area work on enhancing the speed, reliability, and energy efficiency of next-generation very-large-scale integrated (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VLSI" title="VLSI">VLSI</a>) circuits and microsystems. An example of this specialty is work done on reducing the power consumption of VLSI algorithms and architecture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCCC_10-8"><a href="#cite_note-SCCC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Signal.2C_image_and_speech_processing">Signal, image and speech processing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Signal, image and speech processing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Signal_processing" title="Signal processing">Signal processing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Image_processing" title="Image processing">Image processing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Speech_processing" title="Speech processing">Speech processing</a></div>
<p>Computer engineers in this area develop improvements in human–computer interaction, including speech recognition and synthesis, medical and scientific imaging, or communications systems. Other work in this area includes computer vision development such as recognition of human facial features.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCCC_10-9"><a href="#cite_note-SCCC-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most entry-level computer engineering jobs require at least a bachelor's degree in computer engineering. Sometimes a degree in <a href="/wiki/Electronic_engineering" title="Electronic engineering">electronic engineering</a> is accepted, due to the similarity of the two fields. Because hardware engineers commonly work with computer software systems, a background in computer programming usually is needed. According to BLS, "a computer engineering major is similar to electrical engineering but with some computer science courses added to the curriculum".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHE_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-CHE-9">[9]</a></sup> Some large firms or specialized jobs require a master's degree.</p>
<p>It is also important for computer engineers to keep up with rapid advances in technology. Therefore, many continue learning throughout their careers. This can be helpful, especially when it comes to learning new skills or improving existing ones. For example, as the relative cost of fixing a bug increases the further along it is in the software development cycle, there can be greater cost savings attributed to developing and testing for quality code as soon as possible in the process, and particularly before release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_cost_of_fixing_a_bug_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_cost_of_fixing_a_bug-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Job_outlook_in_the_United_States">Job outlook in the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Job outlook in the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_software_engineering_2">Computer software engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Computer software engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Labor_Statistics" title="Bureau of Labor Statistics">Bureau of Labor Statistics</a> (BLS), "computer applications software engineers and computer systems software engineers are projected to be among the faster than average growing occupations" from 2014-24, with a projected growth rate of 17%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> This is down from the 2012 to 2022 BLS estimate of 22% for software developers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-C.S.E._8-1"><a href="#cite_note-C.S.E.-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> And, further down from the 30% 2010 to 2020 BLS estimate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSE2_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-CSE2-14">[14]</a></sup> In addition, growing concerns over cyber security add up to put computer software engineering high above the average rate of increase for all fields. However, some of the work will be outsourced in foreign countries. Due to this, job growth will not be as fast as during the last decade, as jobs that would have gone to computer software engineers in the United States would instead go to computer software engineers in countries such as India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bls.gov_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-bls.gov-15">[15]</a></sup> In addition the BLS Job Outlook for Computer Programmers, 2014-24 has an -8% (a decline in their words)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bls.gov_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-bls.gov-15">[15]</a></sup> for those who program computers (i.e. embedded systems) who are not computer application developers.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_hardware_engineering_2">Computer hardware engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Computer hardware engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Labor_Statistics" title="Bureau of Labor Statistics">BLS</a>, Job Outlook employment for computer hardware engineers, 2014-24 is 3% ("Slower than average" in their own words when compared to other occupations)"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and is down from 7% for 2012 to 2022 BLS estimate<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and is further down from 9% in the BLS 2010 to 2020 estimate." Today, computer hardware is somehow equal to Electronic and Computer Engineering (ECE) and has divided to many subcategories, the most significant of them is Embedded system design.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHE_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-CHE-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
