<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Victor Basili</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>Victor R. Basili</b>, born April 13, 1940 in Brooklyn, New York,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an Emeritus Professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland_Department_of_Computer_Science" title="University of Maryland Department of Computer Science">Department of Computer Science</a>, which is part of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland_College_of_Computer,_Mathematical,_and_Natural_Sciences" title="University of Maryland College of Computer, Mathematical, and Natural Sciences">University of Maryland College of Computer, Mathematical, and Natural Sciences</a>, and the Institute for Advanced Computer Studies. He holds a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ph.D." title="Ph.D.">Ph.D.</a> in Computer Science from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_at_Austin" title="University of Texas at Austin">University of Texas at Austin</a> and two honorary degrees. He is a <i>Fellow</i> of both the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) and of the Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers (IEEE).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1982 through 1988 he was <i>Chair</i> of the Department of Computer Science at the University of Maryland. He is currently a Senior Research Fellow at the <a class="external text" href="http://fc-md.umd.edu/fcmd/index.html" rel="nofollow">Fraunhofer Center for Experimental Software Engineering - Maryland</a> and from 1997-2004 was its Executive Director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is well known for his works on measuring, evaluating, and improving the <a href="/wiki/Software_development_process" title="Software development process">software development process</a>, as a pioneer of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empirical_software_engineering" title="Empirical software engineering">empirical software engineering</a>, especially through his papers on the <a href="/wiki/GQM" title="GQM">Goal/Question/Metric Approach</a>, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Quality_Improvement_Paradigm&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Quality Improvement Paradigm (page does not exist)">Quality Improvement Paradigm</a>, and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Experience_Factory&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Experience Factory (page does not exist)">Experience Factory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Many of these ideas developed through his affiliation with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NASA_Goddard_Space_Flight_Center" title="NASA Goddard Space Flight Center">NASA</a> Goddard Space Flight Center Software Engineering Laboratory (SEL), which he helped to create and was one of its directors from 1976 through 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
