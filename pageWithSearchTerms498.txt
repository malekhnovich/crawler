A <a href="/wiki/Runway_(fashion)" title="Runway (fashion)">catwalk</a> (or <i>fashion runway</i>) is the popular place for showcasing fashion design, especially at <a href="/wiki/Fashion_show" title="Fashion show">fashion shows</a>. Catwalk at <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Fashion_Week" title="Berlin Fashion Week">Berlin Fashion Week</a> for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Michalsky" title="Michael Michalsky">Michalsky's</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/StyleNite" title="StyleNite">StyleNite</a>.</div>

<p><b>Fashion design</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a> of application of <a href="/wiki/Design" title="Design">design</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aesthetics" title="Aesthetics">aesthetics</a> or natural beauty to <a href="/wiki/Clothing" title="Clothing">clothing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fashion_accessory" title="Fashion accessory">accessories</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fashion" title="Fashion">Fashion</a> design is influenced by cultural and social attitudes, and has varied over time and place. Fashion designers work in a number of ways in designing clothing and accessories such as bracelets and necklace. Because of the time required to bring a garment onto the market, designers must at times <a href="/wiki/Fashion_forecasting" title="Fashion forecasting">anticipate changes to consumer tastes</a>.</p>
<p>"Designers conduct research on fashion trends and interpret them for their audience".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> They attempt to design clothes which are functional as well as aesthetically pleasing. They consider who is likely to wear a garment and the situations in which it will be worn. They have a wide range and combinations of materials to work with and a wide range of colors, patterns and styles to choose from. Though most clothing worn for everyday wear falls within a narrow range of conventional styles, unusual garments are usually sought for special occasions such as <a href="/wiki/Formal_wear" title="Formal wear">evening wear</a> or party <a href="/wiki/Dress" title="Dress">dresses</a>.</p>
<p>Some clothes are made specifically for an individual, as in the case of <a href="/wiki/Haute_couture" title="Haute couture">haute couture</a> or <a href="/wiki/Bespoke_tailoring" title="Bespoke tailoring">bespoke tailoring</a>. Today, most clothing is designed for the <a href="/wiki/Mass_market" title="Mass market">mass market</a>, especially casual and every-day wear are called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ready_to_wear" title="Ready to wear">ready to wear</a>.</p>

<p>Fashion designers may work full-time for one fashion house, as 'in-house designers', which owns the designs. They may work alone or as part of a team. Freelance designers work for themselves, selling their designs to fashion houses, directly to shops, or to clothing manufacturers. The garments bear the buyer's label. Some fashion designers set up their own labels, under which their designs are marketed. Some fashion designers are self-employed and design for individual clients. Other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High-end" title="High-end">high-end</a> fashion designers cater to specialty stores or high-end fashion department stores. These designers create original garments, as well as those that follow established fashion trends. Most fashion designers, however, work for apparel manufacturers, creating designs of men’s, women’s, and children’s fashions for the mass market. Large designer brands which have a 'name' as their brand such as <a href="/wiki/Abercrombie_%26_Fitch" title="Abercrombie &amp; Fitch">Abercrombie &amp; Fitch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tween_Brands" title="Tween Brands">Justice</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Juicy_Couture" title="Juicy Couture">Juicy</a> are likely to be designed by a team of individual designers under the direction of a design director.</p>

<p>Fashion designers work in different ways. Some sketch their ideas on paper, while others drape fabric on a dress form. When a designer is completely satisfied with the fit of the <i>toile</i> (or muslin), he or she will consult a professional pattern maker who then makes the finished, working version of the pattern out of card or via a computerized system. The pattern maker's job is very precise and painstaking. The fit of the finished garment depends on their accuracy. Finally, a sample garment is made up and tested on a model to make sure it is an operational outfit.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_fashion_design" title="History of fashion design">History of fashion design</a></div>
<p>Fashion design is generally considered to have started in the 19th century with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Frederick_Worth" title="Charles Frederick Worth">Charles Frederick Worth</a> who was the first designer to have his <a href="/wiki/Label" title="Label">label</a> sewn into the garments that he created. Before the former <a href="/wiki/Draper" title="Draper">draper</a> set up his <i>maison couture</i> (fashion house) in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, clothing design and creation was handled by largely anonymous seamstresses, and high fashion descended from that worn at royal courts. Worth's success was such that he was able to dictate to his customers what they should wear, instead of following their lead as earlier dressmakers had done. The term <i>couturier</i> was in fact first created in order to describe him. While all articles of clothing from any time period are studied by academics as costume design, only clothing created after 1858 is considered as fashion design.</p>
<p>It was during this period that many design houses began to hire artists to sketch or paint designs for garments. The images were shown to clients, which was much cheaper than producing an actual sample garment in the workroom. If the client liked their design, they ordered it and the resulting garment made money for the house. Thus, the tradition of designers sketching out garment designs instead of presenting completed garments on models to customers began as an economy.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_fashion">Types of fashion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Types of fashion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The garments produced by clothing manufacturers fall into three main categories, although these may be split up into additional, more specific categories</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Haute_couture">Haute couture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Haute couture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Haute_couture" title="Haute couture">Haute couture</a></div>
<p>Until the 1950s, fashion clothing was predominately designed and manufactured on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Made-to-measure" title="Made-to-measure">made-to-measure</a> or <a href="/wiki/Haute_couture" title="Haute couture">haute couture</a> basis (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> for high-sewing), with each garment being created for a specific client. A couture garment is made to order for an individual customer, and is usually made from high-quality, expensive fabric, sewn with extreme attention to detail and finish, often using time-consuming, hand-executed techniques. Look and fit take priority over the cost of materials and the time it takes to make.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Due to the high cost of each garment, haute couture makes little direct profit for the fashion houses, but is important for prestige and publicity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ready-to-wear_.28pret-a-porter.29">Ready-to-wear (pret-a-porter)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ready-to-wear (pret-a-porter)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ready-to-wear" title="Ready-to-wear">Ready-to-wear</a></div>
<p>Ready-to-wear clothes are a cross between haute couture and mass market. They are not made for individual customers, but great care is taken in the choice and cut of the fabric. Clothes are made in small quantities to guarantee exclusivity, so they are rather expensive. Ready-to-wear collections are usually presented by fashion houses each season during a period known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fashion_Week" title="Fashion Week">Fashion Week</a>. This takes place on a city-wide basis and occurs twice a year. The main seasons of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fashion_Week" title="Fashion Week">Fashion Week</a> include, spring/summer, fall/winter, resort, swim, and bridal.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mass_market">Mass market</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mass market">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mass_market" title="Mass market">Mass market</a></div>
<p>Currently the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fashion_industry" title="Fashion industry">fashion industry</a> relies more on mass market sales. The mass market caters for a wide range of customers, producing ready-to-wear garments using trends set by the famous names in fashion. They often wait around a season to make sure a style is going to catch on before producing their own versions of the original look. In order to save money and time, they use cheaper fabrics and simpler production techniques which can easily be done by machine. The end product can therefore be sold much more cheaply.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>There is a type of design called "kutch" design originated from the German word "kitschig" meaning "ugly" or "not aesthetically pleasing." <i>Kitsch</i> can also refer to "wearing or displaying something that is therefore no longer in fashion."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>Median annual wages for salaried fashion designers were $61,160 in May 2008. The middle 50 percent earned between $42,150 and $87,120.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The lowest 10 percent earned less than $32,150, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $124,780. Median annual earnings were $52,860 (£28,340) in apparel, piece goods, and notions - the industry employing the largest numbers of fashion designers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>There are many universities that offer fashion design throughout the United States, usually within the context of a general liberal arts degree. The major concentration incorporating fashion design may have alternative names like Apparel and Textiles or Apparel and Textile Design, and may be housed in departments such as Art and Art History, or Family and Consumer Studies. Some schools, such as Parsons, offer a major in Fashion Management, combining fashion education with business courses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_fashion_industry">World fashion industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: World fashion industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fashion today is a global industry, and most major countries have a fashion industry. <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> are the biggest manufacturers of clothing, and other notable clothing manufacturing countries are <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.</p>
<p>The "big four" centres of the fashion industry are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris,_France" title="Paris, France">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. Seven countries have established an international reputation in fashion: <a href="/wiki/French_fashion" title="French fashion">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_fashion" title="Italian fashion">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_fashion" title="German fashion">Germany</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Fashion" title="Japanese Fashion">Japan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_fashion_design">American fashion design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: American fashion design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Fashion_in_the_United_States" title="Fashion in the United States">Fashion in the United States</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Most American fashion houses are based in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, with a high concentration centered in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garment_District,_New_York_City" title="Garment District, New York City">Garment District</a> neighborhood. On the west coast, there are also a significant number of fashion houses in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, where a substantial percentage of high fashion clothing manufactured in the United States is actually made. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills" title="Beverly Hills">Beverly Hills</a>, particularly on <a href="/wiki/Rodeo_Drive" title="Rodeo Drive">Rodeo Drive</a>, is globally renowned for its fashion design and prestigious shopping. Burgeoning industries in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, and especially <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> have developed as well. A semi-annual event held every February and September, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Fashion_Week" title="New York Fashion Week">New York Fashion Week</a> is one of four major <a href="/wiki/Fashion_week" title="Fashion week">fashion weeks</a> held throughout the world. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parsons_The_New_School_for_Design" title="Parsons The New School for Design">Parsons The New School for Design</a>, located in the <a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Village" title="Greenwich Village">Greenwich Village</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Manhattan" title="Lower Manhattan">Lower Manhattan</a> in New York City, is considered as one of the top fashion schools in the world.</p>
<p>American fashion design is dominated by a clean-cut, urban, casual style; reflecting the athletic, health-conscious lifestyles of urban city-dwellers. A designer who helped to set the trend in the United States for sport-influenced day wear throughout the 1940s and 50s was <a href="/wiki/Claire_McCardell" title="Claire McCardell">Claire McCardell</a>. Many of her designs have been revived in recent decades. Famous American brands and designers include <a href="/wiki/Vera_Wang" title="Vera Wang">Vera Wang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Blass" title="Bill Blass">Bill Blass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Klein_(fashion_designer)" title="Calvin Klein (fashion designer)">Calvin Klein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bob_Mackie" title="Bob Mackie">Bob Mackie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Sui" title="Anna Sui">Anna Sui</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Lauren" title="Ralph Lauren">Ralph Lauren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oscar_de_la_Renta" title="Oscar de la Renta">Oscar de la Renta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carolina_Herrera_(fashion_designer)" title="Carolina Herrera (fashion designer)">Carolina Herrera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kors" title="Michael Kors">Michael Kors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Jacobs" title="Marc Jacobs">Marc Jacobs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Ford" title="Tom Ford">Tom Ford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Hilfiger" title="Tommy Hilfiger">Tommy Hilfiger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Beene" title="Geoffrey Beene">Geoffrey Beene</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Cassini" title="Oleg Cassini">Oleg Cassini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perry_Ellis" title="Perry Ellis">Perry Ellis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Cole_(designer)" title="Kenneth Cole (designer)">Kenneth Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Galanos" title="James Galanos">James Galanos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Todd_Oldham" title="Todd Oldham">Todd Oldham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donna_Karan" title="Donna Karan">Donna Karan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liz_Claiborne_(fashion_designer)" title="Liz Claiborne (fashion designer)">Liz Claiborne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nolan_Miller" title="Nolan Miller">Nolan Miller</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Belgian_Fashion_design">Belgian Fashion design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Belgian Fashion design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the late 80s and early 90s, Belgian fashion designers brought a new fashion image that mixed East and West, and brought a highly individualised, personal vision on fashion. Well known Belgian Designers are the <a href="/wiki/Antwerp_Six" title="Antwerp Six">Antwerp Six</a> : <a href="/wiki/Ann_Demeulemeester" title="Ann Demeulemeester">Ann Demeulemeester</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dries_Van_Noten" title="Dries Van Noten">Dries Van Noten</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Bikkembergs" title="Dirk Bikkembergs">Dirk Bikkembergs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dirk_Van_Saene" title="Dirk Van Saene">Dirk Van Saene</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walter_Van_Beirendonck" title="Walter Van Beirendonck">Walter Van Beirendonck</a> and Marina Yee, as well as <a href="/wiki/Martin_Margiela" title="Martin Margiela">Maison Martin Margiela</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raf_Simons" title="Raf Simons">Raf Simons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Pieters" title="Bruno Pieters">Bruno Pieters</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_fashion_design">British fashion design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: British fashion design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> has long been the capital of the United Kingdom fashion industry and has a wide range of foreign designs which have integrated with modern British styles. Typical, British design is smart but innovative yet recently has become more and more unconventional, fusing traditional styles with modern techniques. Vintage styles play an important role in the British fashion and styling industry. Stylists regularly 'mix and match' the old with the new, which gives British style that unique, bohemian aesthetic that many of the other fashion capitals try to imitate. Irish fashion (both design and styling) is also heavily influenced by fashion trends from Britain. Famous British brands and designers include <a href="/wiki/Burberry" title="Burberry">Burberry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Smith_(fashion_designer)" title="Paul Smith (fashion designer)">Paul Smith</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfred_Dunhill,_Ltd." title="Alfred Dunhill, Ltd.">Alfred Dunhill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_McQueen_(brand)" title="Alexander McQueen (brand)">Alexander McQueen</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Galliano" title="John Galliano">John Galliano</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Richmond_(fashion_designer)" title="John Richmond (fashion designer)">John Richmond</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neil_Barrett_(fashion_designer)" title="Neil Barrett (fashion designer)">Neil Barrett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Williamson" title="Matthew Williamson">Matthew Williamson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hussein_Chalayan" title="Hussein Chalayan">Hussein Chalayan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Pugh" title="Gareth Pugh">Gareth Pugh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stella_McCartney" title="Stella McCartney">Stella McCartney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mulberry_(company)" title="Mulberry (company)">Mulberry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Pink" title="Thomas Pink">Thomas Pink</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vivienne_Westwood" title="Vivienne Westwood">Vivienne Westwood</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<p>Toronto holds annual <a href="/wiki/Fashion_show" title="Fashion show">fashion shows</a> to showcase what its fashion designers have to offer.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="French_fashion_design">French fashion design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: French fashion design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/French_fashion" title="French fashion">French fashion</a></div>
<p>Most French fashion houses are in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, which is the capital of <a href="/wiki/French_fashion" title="French fashion">French fashion</a>. Traditionally, French fashion is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chic_(style)" title="Chic (style)">chic</a> and stylish, defined by its sophistication, cut, and smart accessories. French fashion is internationally acclaimed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="German_fashion_design">German fashion design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: German fashion design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/German_fashion" title="German fashion">German fashion</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> is the center of fashion in Germany (prominently displayed at <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Fashion_Week" title="Berlin Fashion Week">Berlin Fashion Week</a>), while <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a> holds Europe's largest fashion trade fairs with <i>Igedo</i>. Other important centers of the scene are <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>. <a href="/wiki/German_fashion" title="German fashion">German fashion</a> is famed for its elegant lines as well as unconventional young designs and the great variety of styles.</p>
<p>Globally renowned fashion designers from Germany are <a href="/wiki/Karl_Lagerfeld" title="Karl Lagerfeld">Karl Lagerfeld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Boss_(fashion_designer)" title="Hugo Boss (fashion designer)">Hugo Boss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Joop" title="Wolfgang Joop">Wolfgang Joop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jil_Sander" title="Jil Sander">Jil Sander</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Michalsky" title="Michael Michalsky">Michael Michalsky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Etienne_Aigner" title="Etienne Aigner">Etienne Aigner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rudolph_Moshammer" title="Rudolph Moshammer">Rudolph Moshammer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Torsten_Amft" title="Torsten Amft">Torsten Amft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Willy_Bogner,_Jr." title="Willy Bogner, Jr.">Willy Bogner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philipp_Plein" title="Philipp Plein">Philipp Plein</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stephanie_Hahn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stephanie Hahn (page does not exist)">Stephanie Hahn</a> .</p>
<p>Famous fashion brands include <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=22/4_HOMMES_FEMMES&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="22/4 HOMMES FEMMES (page does not exist)">22/4_HOMMES_FEMMES</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Boss" title="Hugo Boss">Hugo Boss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">adidas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puma_SE" title="Puma SE">PUMA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Escada" title="Escada">Escada</a>, <a href="/wiki/JOOP!" title="JOOP!">JOOP!</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_Creation_Munich" title="Modern Creation Munich">MCM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Banani" title="Bruno Banani">Bruno Banani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jil_Sander_(brand)" title="Jil Sander (brand)">Jil Sander</a>, <a href="/wiki/Triumph_International" title="Triumph International">Triumph International</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schiesser" title="Schiesser">Schiesser</a>, <a href="/wiki/Closed_GmbH" title="Closed GmbH">Closed GmbH</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reusch_(company)" title="Reusch (company)">Reusch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valisere" title="Valisere">Valisere</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Wolfskin" title="Jack Wolfskin">Jack Wolfskin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ulla_Popken" title="Ulla Popken">Ulla Popken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_(footwear)" title="Buffalo (footwear)">Buffalo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rohde_Shoes" title="Rohde Shoes">Rohde Shoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zalando" title="Zalando">Zalando</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Martens" title="Dr. Martens">Dr. Martens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peek_%26_Cloppenburg" title="Peek &amp; Cloppenburg">P&amp;C</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_O%27Polo" title="Marc O'Polo">Marc O'Polo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Tailor" title="Tom Tailor">Tom Tailor</a>, <a href="/wiki/S.Oliver" title="S.Oliver">s.Oliver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Esprit_Holdings" title="Esprit Holdings">Esprit</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wunderkind_(fashion)" title="Wunderkind (fashion)">Wunderkind</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italian_fashion_design">Italian fashion design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Italian fashion design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Italian_fashion" title="Italian fashion">Italian fashion</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> is Italy's fashion capital. Most of the older Italian couturiers are in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>. However, Milan and <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a> are the <a href="/wiki/Italian_fashion" title="Italian fashion">Italian fashion</a> capitals, and it is the exhibition venue for their collections. Italian fashion features casual and glamorous elegance.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japanese_fashion_design">Japanese fashion design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Japanese fashion design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Japan" title="Culture of Japan">Japanese</a> fashion houses are in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>. The Japanese look is loose and unstructured (often resulting from complicated cutting), colours tend to the sombre and subtle, and richly textured fabrics. Famous Japanese designers include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kenzo_Takada" title="Kenzo Takada">Kenzo Takada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Issey_Miyake" title="Issey Miyake">Issey Miyake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yohji_Yamamoto" title="Yohji Yamamoto">Yohji Yamamoto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rei_Kawakubo" title="Rei Kawakubo">Rei Kawakubo</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chinese_fashion_design">Chinese fashion design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Chinese fashion design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hong Kong clothing brand <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_Tang" title="Shanghai Tang">Shanghai Tang</a>'s design concept is inspired by Chinese clothing and set out to rejuvenate Chinese fashion of the 1920s and 30s, with a modern twist of the 21st century and its usage of bright colours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> As of today, there are many fashion designers in the region of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soviet_fashion_design">Soviet fashion design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Soviet fashion design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Fashion_in_the_Soviet_Union" title="Fashion in the Soviet Union">Fashion in the Soviet Union</a></div>
<p>Fashion in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> largely followed general trends of the Western world. However, the state’s <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialist ideology</a> consistently moderated and influenced these trends. In addition, shortages of <a href="/wiki/Consumer_goods_in_the_Soviet_Union" title="Consumer goods in the Soviet Union">consumer goods</a> meant that the general public did not have ready access to pre-made fashion.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Swiss_fashion_design">Swiss fashion design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Swiss fashion design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most of the Swiss fashion houses are in <a href="/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich" title="Zürich">Zürich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The Swiss look is casual elegant and luxurious with a slight touch of quirkiness. Additionally, it has been greatly influenced by the dance club scene.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fashion_design_terms">Fashion design terms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashion_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Fashion design terms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>A <b>fashion designer</b> conceives garment combinations of line, proportion, color, and texture. While sewing and pattern-making skills are beneficial, they are not a pre-requisite of successful fashion design. Most fashion designers are formally trained or apprenticed.</li>
<li>A <b>technical designer</b> works with the design team and the factories overseas to ensure correct garment construction, appropriate fabric choices and a good fit. The technical designer fits the garment samples on a fit model, and decides which fit and construction changes to make before mass-producing the garment.</li>
<li>A <b><a href="/wiki/Pattern_(sewing)" title="Pattern (sewing)">pattern maker</a></b> (or <i>pattern cutter</i>) drafts the shapes and sizes of a garment's pieces. This may be done manually with paper and measuring tools or by using a CAD computer software program. Another method is to drape fabric directly onto a dress form. The resulting pattern pieces can be constructed to produce the intended design of the garment and required size. Formal training is usually required for working as a pattern marker.</li>
<li>A <b><a href="/wiki/Tailor" title="Tailor">tailor</a></b> makes custom designed garments made to the client's measure; especially suits (coat and trousers, jacket and skirt, et cetera). Tailors usually undergo an apprenticeship or other formal training.</li>
<li>A <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Textiles" title="Textiles">textile designer</a></b> designs fabric weaves and prints for clothes and furnishings. Most textile designers are formally trained as apprentices and in school.</li>
<li>A <b><a href="/wiki/Wardrobe_stylist" title="Wardrobe stylist">stylist</a></b> co-ordinates the clothes, jewelry, and accessories used in fashion photography and catwalk presentations. A stylist may also work with an individual client to design a coordinated wardrobe of garments. Many stylists are trained in fashion design, the history of fashion, and historical costume, and have a high level of expertise in the current fashion market and future market trends. However, some simply have a strong aesthetic sense for pulling great looks together.</li>
<li>A <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fashion_buyer" title="Fashion buyer">fashion buyer</a></b> selects and buys the mix of clothing available in retail shops, department stores, and chain stores. Most fashion buyers are trained in business and/or fashion studies.</li>
<li>A <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seamstress" title="Seamstress">seamstress</a></b> sews ready-to-wear or mass-produced clothing by hand or with a sewing machine, either in a garment shop or as a sewing machine operator in a factory. She (or he) may not have the skills to make (design and cut) the garments, or to fit them on a model.</li>
<li>A <b><a href="/wiki/Teacher" title="Teacher">teacher of fashion design</a></b> teaches the art and craft of fashion design in art or fashion school.</li>
<li>A <b>custom clothier</b> makes custom-made garments to order, for a given customer.</li>
<li>A <b><a href="/wiki/Dressmaker" title="Dressmaker">dressmaker</a></b> specializes in custom-made women's clothes: day, cocktail, and evening dresses, business clothes and suits, trousseaus, sports clothes, and lingerie.</li>
<li>An <b><a href="/wiki/Illustration" title="Illustration">illustrator</a></b> draws and paints clothing designs for commercial use.</li>
<li>A <b><a href="/wiki/Fashion_forecasting" title="Fashion forecasting">fashion forecaster</a></b> predicts what colours, styles and shapes will be popular ("on-trend") before the garments are on sale in stores.</li>
<li>A <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fashion_model" title="Fashion model">model</a></b> wears and displays clothes at fashion shows and in photographs.</li>
<li>A <b><a href="/wiki/Fit_model" title="Fit model">fit model</a></b> aids the fashion designer by wearing and commenting on the fit of clothes during their design and pre-manufacture. Fit models need to be a particular size for this purpose.</li>
<li>A <b><a href="/wiki/Fashion_journalism" title="Fashion journalism">fashion journalist</a></b> writes fashion articles describing the garments presented or fashion trends, for magazines or newspapers.</li>
<li>An <b>alterations specialist</b> (<b>alterationist</b>) adjusts the fit of completed garments, usually ready-to-wear, and sometimes re-styles them. NOTE: despite tailors altering garments to fit the client, not all alterationists are tailors.</li>
<li>An <b>Image Consultant</b>, <b>wardrobe consultant</b> or <b>fashion advisor</b> recommends styles and colors that are flattering to the client.</li>
</ul>
