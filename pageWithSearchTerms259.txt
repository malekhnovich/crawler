<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Enterprise Unified Process</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="hatnote" role="note">For other uses, see <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Eup_(disambiguation)" title="Eup (disambiguation)">Eup (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>Enterprise Unified Process</b> (<b>EUP</b>) is an extended variant of the <a href="/wiki/Rational_Unified_Process" title="Rational Unified Process">Unified Process</a> and was developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scott_W._Ambler" title="Scott W. Ambler">Scott W. Ambler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Larry_Constantine" title="Larry Constantine">Larry Constantine</a> in 2000, eventually reworked in 2005 by Ambler, John Nalbone and <a class="external text" href="http://www.michaelvizdos.com" rel="nofollow">Michael Vizdos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> EUP was originally introduced to overcome some shortages of RUP, namely the lack of production and eventual retirement of a software system. So two phases and several new disciplines were added. EUP sees software development not as a standalone activity, but embedded in the lifecycle of the system (to be built or enhanced or replaced), the IT lifecycle of the enterprise and the organization/business lifecycle of the enterprise itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It deals with software development as seen from the customer's point of view.</p>
<p>In 2013 work began to evolve EUP to be based on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disciplined_Agile_Delivery" title="Disciplined Agile Delivery">Disciplined Agile Delivery</a> instead of the Unified Process.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Phases">Phases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enterprise_Unified_Process&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Phases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Unified Process defines four project phases</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Inception" title="Inception">Inception</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Elaboration" title="Elaboration">Elaboration</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">Construction</a></li>
<li>Transition</li>
</ul>
<p>To these EUP adds two additional phases</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_manufacturing" title="Software manufacturing">Production</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Retirement" title="Retirement">Retirement</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Disciplines">Disciplines</span><i><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enterprise_Unified_Process&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Disciplines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></i></h2>
<p>The Rational Unified Process defines nine project disciplines</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Business_process_modeling" title="Business process modeling">Business Modeling</a></li>
<li>Requirements</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Analysis_and_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Analysis and Design (page does not exist)">Analysis and Design</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Implementation" title="Implementation">Implementation</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Test_(assessment)" title="Test (assessment)">Test</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_deployment" title="Software deployment">Deployment</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Configuration_and_Change_Management&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Configuration and Change Management (page does not exist)">Configuration and Change Management</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Project_Management" title="Project Management">Project Management</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">Environment</a></li>
</ul>
<p>To these EUP adds one additional project discipline</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Operations_and_Support&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Operations and Support (page does not exist)">Operations and Support</a></li>
</ul>
<p>and seven enterprise disciplines</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Enterprise_Business_Modeling&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Enterprise Business Modeling (page does not exist)">Enterprise Business Modeling</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Investment_management" title="Investment management">Portfolio Management</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Enterprise_architecture" title="Enterprise architecture">Enterprise Architecture</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Strategic_Reuse&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Strategic Reuse (page does not exist)">Strategic Reuse</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/People_Management" title="People Management">People Management</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Enterprise_Administration&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Enterprise Administration (page does not exist)">Enterprise Administration</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Software_Process_Improvement&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Software Process Improvement (page does not exist)">Software Process Improvement</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Best_Practices_of_EUP">Best Practices of EUP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enterprise_Unified_Process&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Best Practices of EUP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The EUP provide following best practices:-</p>
<ol>
<li>Develop iteratively</li>
<li>Manage requirements</li>
<li>Proven architecture</li>
<li>Modeling</li>
<li>Continuously verify quality.</li>
<li>Manage change</li>
<li>Collaborative development</li>
<li>Look beyond development.</li>
<li>Deliver working software on a regular basis</li>
<li>Manage risk</li>
</ol>
