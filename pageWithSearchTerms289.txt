<p>Fayad received his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a> in Agriculture Engineering from <a href="/wiki/Cairo_University" title="Cairo University">Cairo University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>. He received his Master's and Ph.D. in computer science from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Minnesota" title="University of Minnesota">University of Minnesota</a> at <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a>, where his research topic was Object Oriented Software Engineering: Problems &amp; Perspectives. He lives in California.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Fayad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fayad was associate professor at the computer science and computer engineering faculty at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Nevada" title="University of Nevada">University of Nevada</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reno" title="Reno">Reno</a>, from 1995 - 1999 and J.D. Edwards Professor,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Computer Science &amp; Engineering, at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Nebraska,_Lincoln" title="University of Nebraska, Lincoln">University of Nebraska, Lincoln</a>, from 1999 to 2002. Dr. Fayad has more than fifteen years of industrial experience<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> that include ten years as a software architect in many companies, such as <a href="/wiki/McDonnell_Douglas" title="McDonnell Douglas">McDonnell Douglas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philips_Natuurkundig_Laboratorium" title="Philips Natuurkundig Laboratorium">Philips Research Laboratory</a>.</p>
<p>He is the lead author of several classic <a href="/wiki/John_Wiley_%26_Sons" title="John Wiley &amp; Sons">Wiley</a> books: Transition to Object Oriented Software Development, August 1998, Building Application Frameworks, Sept., 1999, Implementing Application Frameworks, Sept., 1999, Domain-Specific Application Frameworks, Oct., 1999, and several CRC Press, Taylor &amp; Francis Group: Software Patterns, Knowledge Maps, and Domain Analysis, December 2014. He is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IEEE" title="IEEE">IEEE</a> Distinguished Speaker, an Associate Editor, Editorial Advisor, a columnist for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Communications_of_the_ACM&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Communications of the ACM (page does not exist)">The Communications of the ACM</a>, where his column is <a class="external text" href="http://dl.acm.org/citation.cfm?id=383713," rel="nofollow">Thinking Objectively</a>, a columnist for <a href="/wiki/Al-Ahram" title="Al-Ahram">Al-Ahram</a>, Egyptians Newspaper, an Editor-In-Chief for IEEE Computer Society Press - Computer Science and Engineering Practice Press (1995-1997), a general chair of IEEE/Arab Computer Society International Conference on Computer Systems and Applications, <i><a class="external text" href="http://www.lau.edu.lb/news-events/conferences/aiccsa2001/contact.html" rel="nofollow">AICCSA 2001</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Beirut" title="Beirut">Beirut</a>, Lebanon, 2001, and the founder and president of Arab Computer Society, <i><a class="external text" href="http://www.arabcomputersociety.org/index.htm" rel="nofollow">ACS</a></i> from April 2004 to April 2007. He has been a Full Professor of Computer Engineering at San Jose State University since 2002.</p>
