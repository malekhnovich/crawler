<p><b>Craig Larman</b> (born ca. 1958) is a <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> specializing in <a href="/wiki/Iterative_and_incremental_development" title="Iterative and incremental development">Iterative and incremental development</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agile_software_development" title="Agile software development">Agile software development</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Object-oriented_analysis" title="Object-oriented analysis">Object-oriented analysis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_design" title="Object-oriented design">Object-oriented design</a>, and agile modeling. He is the author of several texts on large scale <a href="/wiki/Scrum_(software_development)" title="Scrum (software development)">Scrum</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craig_Larman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Larman received his B.Sc. and in 1985 his M.Sc., both in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/Simon_Fraser_University" title="Simon Fraser University">Simon Fraser University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, where he got familiarized with the fields of <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_programming" title="Object-oriented programming">object-oriented programming</a>, <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Case-based_reasoning" title="Case-based reasoning">case-based reasoning</a>.</p>
<p>During his studies Larman started working as independent product developer in 1978 in Vancouver. After graduation in 1985 he switched to Organizational Design consultancy. Currently, he is chief scientist at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Valtech&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Valtech (page does not exist)">Valtech</a>, a technology consulting and skills transfer organization based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris,_France" title="Paris, France">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.</p>
<p>Larman also serves as an independent author<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ProtectedVariation_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ProtectedVariation-1">[1]</a></sup> and consultant with a focus on organizational redesign and systems thinking<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IEEEComputerSociety_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-IEEEComputerSociety-2">[2]</a></sup> for businesses and organizations adopting iterative and agile practices, agile modeling, or OOA/D, and he has been helpful in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forensic" title="Forensic">forensic</a> investigations with failed software projects and as an expert witness in legal disputes.</p>
