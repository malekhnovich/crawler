<p><b>Model-driven engineering</b> (<b>MDE</b>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Software_development_methodology" title="Software development methodology">software development methodology</a> that focuses on creating and exploiting <a href="/wiki/Domain_model" title="Domain model">domain models</a>, which are conceptual models of all the topics related to a specific problem. Hence, it highlights and aims at <a href="/wiki/Representation_(mathematics)" title="Representation (mathematics)">abstract representations</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_representation_and_reasoning" title="Knowledge representation and reasoning">knowledge</a> and activities that govern a particular <a href="/wiki/Problem_domain" title="Problem domain">application domain</a>, rather than the computing (f.e. algorithmic) concepts.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Model-driven_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The MDE approach is meant to increase productivity by maximizing compatibility between systems (via reuse of standardized models), simplifying the process of design (via models of recurring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Design_patterns" title="Design patterns">design patterns</a> in the application domain), and promoting communication between individuals and teams working on the system (via a standardization of the terminology and the <a href="/wiki/Best_practice" title="Best practice">best practices</a> used in the application domain).</p>
<p>A modeling paradigm for MDE is considered effective if its models make sense from the point of view of a user that is familiar with the domain, and if they can serve as a basis for implementing systems. The models are developed through extensive communication among product managers, designers, developers and users of the application domain. As the models approach completion, they enable the development of software and systems.</p>
<p>Some of the better known MDE initiatives are:</p>
<ul>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/Object_Management_Group" title="Object Management Group">Object Management Group</a> (OMG) initiative <a href="/wiki/Model-driven_architecture" title="Model-driven architecture">model-driven architecture</a> (MDA), which is a registered trademark of OMG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-omgtm_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-omgtm-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/Eclipse_(software)" title="Eclipse (software)">Eclipse</a> ecosystem of programming and modelling tools (<a href="/wiki/Eclipse_Modeling_Framework" title="Eclipse Modeling Framework">Eclipse Modeling Framework</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Model-driven_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first tools to support MDE were the Computer-Aided Software Engineering (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_Aided_Software_Engineering" title="Computer Aided Software Engineering">CASE</a>) tools developed in the 1980s. Companies like Integrated Development Environments (IDE - StP), Higher Order Software (now Hamilton Technologies, Inc., HTI), Cadre Technologies, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bachman_Information_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bachman Information Systems (page does not exist)">Bachman Information Systems</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Logic_Works" title="Logic Works">Logic Works</a> (BP-Win and ER-Win) were pioneers in the field.</p>
<p>The US government got involved in the modeling definitions creating the <a href="/wiki/IDEF" title="IDEF">IDEF</a> specifications. With several variations of the modeling definitions (see <a href="/wiki/Booch_method" title="Booch method">Booch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Object-modeling_technique" title="Object-modeling technique">Rumbaugh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_software_engineering" title="Object-oriented software engineering">Jacobson</a>, Gane and Sarson, <a href="/wiki/State_diagram#Harel_statechart" title="State diagram">Harel</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shlaer-Mellor" title="Shlaer-Mellor">Shlaer and Mellor</a>, and others) they were eventually joined creating the <a href="/wiki/Unified_Modeling_Language" title="Unified Modeling Language">Unified Modeling Language</a> (UML). <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rational_Rose" title="Rational Rose">Rational Rose</a>, a product for UML implementation, was done by Rational Corporation (Booch) responding automation yield higher levels of abstraction in software development. This abstraction promotes simpler models with a greater focus on problem space. Combined with executable semantics this elevates the total level of automation possible. The <a href="/wiki/Object_Management_Group" title="Object Management Group">Object Management Group</a> (OMG) has developed a set of standards called <a href="/wiki/Model-driven_architecture" title="Model-driven architecture">model-driven architecture</a> (MDA), building a foundation for this advanced architecture-focused approach.</p>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Douglas_C._Schmidt" title="Douglas C. Schmidt">Douglas C. Schmidt</a>, model-driven engineering technologies offer a promising approach to address the inability of third-generation languages to alleviate the complexity of platforms and express domain concepts effectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
