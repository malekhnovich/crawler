<p><b>Software quality assurance</b> (<b>SQA</b>) consists of a means of monitoring the <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a> processes and methods used to ensure quality.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The methods by which this is accomplished are many and varied, and may include ensuring conformance to one or more standards, such as <a href="/wiki/ISO_9000" title="ISO 9000">ISO 9000</a> or a model such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CMMI" title="CMMI">CMMI</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>SQA encompasses the entire <a href="/wiki/Software_development" title="Software development">software development</a> process, which includes processes such as requirements definition, <a href="/wiki/Software_design" title="Software design">software design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_programming" title="Computer programming">coding</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revision_control" title="Revision control">source code control</a>, <a href="/wiki/Code_review" title="Code review">code reviews</a>, <a href="/wiki/Software_configuration_management" title="Software configuration management">software configuration management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Software_testing" title="Software testing">testing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Release_management" title="Release management">release management</a>, and product integration. SQA is organized into goals, commitments, abilities, activities, measurements, and verifications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Software quality assurance, according to <a href="/wiki/ISO/IEC_15504" title="ISO/IEC 15504">ISO/IEC 15504</a> v.2.5 (SPICE), is a supporting process that has to provide the independent assurance in which all the work products, activities and processes comply with the predefined plans and ISO 15504.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
