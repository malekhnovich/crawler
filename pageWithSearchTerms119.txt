<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Jim Coplien</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/w/index.php?title=James_O._Coplien&amp;redirect=no" title="James O. Coplien">James O. Coplien</a>)</span></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>James O. "Jim" Coplien</b> (also known as <b>Cope</b>) is a writer, lecturer, and researcher in the field of <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>. He held the 2003–4 Vloeberghs Leerstoel (Vloeberghs Chair) at <a href="/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit_Brussel" title="Vrije Universiteit Brussel">Vrije Universiteit Brussel</a> and has been a visiting professor at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Manchester" title="University of Manchester">University of Manchester</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jim_Coplien&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His ongoing work with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Liping_Zhao&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Liping Zhao (page does not exist)">Liping Zhao</a> includes a monograph entitled "A Generalized Formal Design Theory" which explores the foundations of symmetry and symmetry-breaking in design in general, and in patterns in particular.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Cope was a founding Member of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hillside_Group" title="Hillside Group">Hillside Group</a> with <a href="/wiki/Kent_Beck" title="Kent Beck">Kent Beck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grady_Booch" title="Grady Booch">Grady Booch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ward_Cunningham" title="Ward Cunningham">Ward Cunningham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Johnson_(computer_scientist)" title="Ralph Johnson (computer scientist)">Ralph Johnson</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ken_Auer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ken Auer (page does not exist)">Ken Auer</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hal_Hildebrand&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hal Hildebrand (page does not exist)">Hal Hildebrand</a>. He has started up several of the conferences in the <a href="/wiki/Pattern_Languages_of_Programs" title="Pattern Languages of Programs">Pattern Languages of Programs</a> (PLoP) conference series and is a longstanding pattern author and PLoP shepherd. His pattern form, the "Coplien Form,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a simplified way to structure a pattern in preparation for writing a more literate version in Alexandrian form. Together with Trygve Reenskaug, he was a principal in the design of the <a href="/wiki/Data,_context_and_interaction" title="Data, context and interaction">data, context and interaction</a> (DCI) paradigm.</p>
<p>He was also Program Chair of Object-Oriented Programming, Systems, Languages &amp; Applications conference (<a href="/wiki/OOPSLA" title="OOPSLA">OOPSLA</a>) in 1996, and has been a co-founder and sometime chair of many software pattern conferences.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jim_Coplien&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His early work on C++ idioms was one of the three primary sources of the popular <i><a href="/wiki/Design_Patterns" title="Design Patterns">Design Patterns</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He also named the <a href="/wiki/Curiously_recurring_template_pattern" title="Curiously recurring template pattern">curiously recurring template pattern</a> C++ idiom.<sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_CRTP"><a class="external autonumber" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jim_Coplien#endnote_CRTP">[1]</a></sup> His work on <a href="/wiki/Organizational_patterns" title="Organizational patterns">organizational patterns</a> was an inspiration for both <a href="/wiki/Extreme_programming" title="Extreme programming">extreme programming</a><sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_XP"><a class="external autonumber" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jim_Coplien#endnote_XP">[2]</a></sup> and for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scrum_(development)" title="Scrum (development)">Scrum</a> daily standups.<sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_OriginsOfScrum"><a class="external autonumber" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jim_Coplien#endnote_OriginsOfScrum">[3]</a></sup> In <i>Organizational Patterns of Agile Software Development</i> book he co-presented an alternative version of <a href="/wiki/Conway%27s_law" title="Conway's law">Conway's law</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Presenter">Presenter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jim_Coplien&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Presenter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Coplien has presented several times in the UK at the ACCU conference:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://accu.org/index.php/conferences/accu_conference_2010/accu2010_sessions#Lean%20Architecture%20and%20Agile%20Software%20Development" rel="nofollow">ACCU2010 Lean Architecture and Agile Software Development</a></li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://accu.org/index.php/conferences/accu_conference_2008/accu2008_sessions#Five%20practical%20solutions%20to%20Agile%20myths" rel="nofollow">ACCU2008 Five practical solutions to Agile myths</a></li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://accu.org/index.php/conferences/accu_conference_2008/accu2008_sessions#Organizational%20Patterns:%20The%20Foundations%20of%20Agile" rel="nofollow">ACCU2008 Organizational Patterns: The Foundations of Agile</a></li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://accu.org/index.php/conferences/accu_conference_2007/accu2007_sessions#A%20balanced%20Agile%20design%20approach" rel="nofollow">ACCU2007 A balanced Agile design approach</a></li>
</ul>
<p>He has given several conference keynotes, including the recent presentations <a class="external text" href="http://splashcon.org/2012/program/403" rel="nofollow">"Reflections on Reflection"</a> at SPLASH 2013, <a class="external text" href="http://scrumgatheringtokyo.org/2013/?page_id=282" rel="nofollow">"Kaizen and Certification"</a> at the 2013 Scrum Alliance Regional Conference in Tokyo, and <a class="external text" href="http://www.aosd.net/2012/program/keynotes-and-lectures.html" rel="nofollow">"Objects of the people, by the people, and for the people"</a> at the AOSD Conference in Berlin in 2012.</p>
