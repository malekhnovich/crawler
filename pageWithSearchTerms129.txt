<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Readers–writer lock</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="hatnote" role="note">For the psychological difficulty sometimes encountered by writers, see <a href="/wiki/Writer%27s_block" title="Writer's block">Writer's block</a>.</div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, a <b>readers–writer</b> (<b>RW</b>) or <b>shared-exclusive</b> lock (also known as a <b>multiple readers/single-writer</b> lock<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hamilton_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hamilton-1">[1]</a></sup> or <b>multi-reader</b> lock<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/Synchronization_(computer_science)" title="Synchronization (computer science)">synchronization</a> primitive that solves one of the <a href="/wiki/Readers%E2%80%93writers_problem" title="Readers–writers problem">readers–writers problems</a>. An RW lock allows <a href="/wiki/Concurrency_control" title="Concurrency control">concurrent</a> access for read-only operations, while write operations require exclusive access. This means that multiple threads can read the data in parallel but an exclusive <a href="/wiki/Lock_(computer_science)" title="Lock (computer science)">lock</a> is needed for writing or modifying data. When a writer is writing the data, all other writers or readers will be blocked until the writer is finished writing. A common use might be to control access to a data structure in memory that cannot be updated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atomicity_(programming)" title="Atomicity (programming)">atomically</a> and is invalid (and should not be read by another thread) until the update is complete.</p>
<p>Readers–writer locks are usually constructed on top of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mutex" title="Mutex">mutexes</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Condition_variable" title="Condition variable">condition variables</a>, or on top of <a href="/wiki/Semaphore_(programming)" title="Semaphore (programming)">semaphores</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Upgradable_RW_lock">Upgradable RW lock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Readers%E2%80%93writer_lock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Upgradable RW lock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some RW locks allow the lock to be atomically upgraded from being locked in read-mode to write-mode, as well as being downgraded from write-mode to read-mode. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.boost.org/doc/html/thread/synchronization.html#thread.synchronization.mutex_concepts.upgrade_lockable" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Priority_policies">Priority policies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Readers%E2%80%93writer_lock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Priority policies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>RW locks can be designed with different priority policies for reader vs. writer access. The lock can either be designed to always give priority to readers (<i>read-preferring</i>), to always give priority to writers (<i>write-preferring</i>) or be <i>unspecified</i> with regards to priority. These policies lead to different tradeoffs with regards to <a href="/wiki/Concurrency_(computer_science)" title="Concurrency (computer science)">concurrency</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Resource_starvation" title="Resource starvation">starvation</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Read-preferring RW locks</i> allow for maximum concurrency, but can lead to write-starvation if contention is high. This is because writer threads will not be able to acquire the lock as long as at least one reading thread holds it. Since multiple reader threads may hold the lock at once, this means that a writer thread may continue waiting for the lock while new reader threads are able to acquire the lock, even to the point where the writer may still be waiting after all of the readers which were holding the lock when it first attempted to acquire it have released the lock. Priority to readers may be <i>weak</i>, as just described, or <i>strong</i>, meaning that whenever a writer releases the lock, any blocking readers always acquire it next.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raynal_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-raynal-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:76</sup></li>
<li><i>Write-preferring RW locks</i> avoid the problem of writer starvation by preventing any <i>new</i> readers from acquiring the lock if there is a writer queued and waiting for the lock; the writer will acquire the lock as soon as all readers which were already holding the lock have completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The downside is that write-preferring locks allows for less concurrency in the presence of writer threads, compared to read-preferring RW locks. Also the lock is less performant because each operation, taking or releasing the lock for either read or write, is more complex, internally requiring taking and releasing two mutexes instead of one.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This variation is sometimes also known as "write-biased" readers–writer lock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><i>Unspecified priority RW locks</i> does not provide any guarantees with regards read vs. write access. Unspecified priority can in some situations be preferable if it allows for a more efficient implementation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Implementation">Implementation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Readers%E2%80%93writer_lock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Implementation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several implementation strategies for readers–writer locks exist, reducing them to synchronization primitives that are assumed to pre-exist.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Using_two_mutexes">Using two mutexes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Readers%E2%80%93writer_lock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Using two mutexes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Michel_Raynal" title="Michel Raynal">Raynal</a> demonstrates how to implement an R/W lock using two mutexes and a single integer counter. The counter, <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">b</span>, tracks the number of blocking readers. One mutex, <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">r</span>, protects <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">b</span> and is only used by readers; the other, <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">g</span> (for "global") ensures mutual exclusion of writers. The following is <a href="/wiki/Pseudocode" title="Pseudocode">pseudocode</a> for the lock-for-read operation:</p>
<div style="margin-left: 35px; width: 600px">
<div style="width:auto ; margin-left: margin-bottom:1.25em;border:1px solid #8898BF; background:transparent;padding:0">
<div style="height:8px;margin:0;border:0;border-bottom:1px solid #8898BF;background: #C8D8FF;font-size:1px"></div>
<div style="padding:5px;font-size:small">
<ul>
<li>Lock <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">r</span>.</li>
<li>Increment <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">b</span>.</li>
<li>If <span class="texhtml"><i>b</i> = 1</span>, lock <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">g</span>.</li>
<li>Unlock <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">r</span>.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Readers use a similar operation for unlocking, that, while holding the lock <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">r</span>, decrements <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">b</span> and unlocks <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">g</span> if this causes <span class="texhtml">b</span> to become equal to zero. Writers simply lock and unlock <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">g</span> directly. This implementation strategy gives weak priority to readers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raynal_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-raynal-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:76</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Using_a_condition_variable_and_a_mutex">Using a condition variable and a mutex</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Readers%E2%80%93writer_lock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Using a condition variable and a mutex">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alternatively, a read-preferring R/W lock can be implemented in terms of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Condition_variable" title="Condition variable">condition variable</a> and an ordinary (mutex) lock, in addition to an integer counter and a boolean flag. The lock-for-read operation in this setup is:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-herlihy_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-herlihy-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pthreads_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-pthreads-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-butenhof_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-butenhof-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<div style="margin-left: 35px; width: 600px">
<div style="width:auto ; margin-left: margin-bottom:1.25em;border:1px solid #8898BF; background:transparent;padding:0">
<div style="height:8px;margin:0;border:0;border-bottom:1px solid #8898BF;background: #C8D8FF;font-size:1px"></div>
<div style="padding:5px;font-size:small">
<ul>
<li>Input: mutex <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">m</span>, condition variable <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">c</span>, integer <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">r</span> (number of readers waiting), flag <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">w</span> (writer waiting).</li>
<li>Lock <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">m</span> (blocking).</li>
<li>While <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">w</span>:
<ul>
<li><span class="texhtml">wait <i>c</i>, <i>m</i></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[a]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Increment <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">r</span>.</li>
<li>Unlock <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">m</span>.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The lock-for-write operation is similar, but slightly different (inputs are the same as for lock-for-read):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-herlihy_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-herlihy-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pthreads_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-pthreads-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-butenhof_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-butenhof-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<div style="margin-left: 35px; width: 600px">
<div style="width:auto ; margin-left: margin-bottom:1.25em;border:1px solid #8898BF; background:transparent;padding:0">
<div style="height:8px;margin:0;border:0;border-bottom:1px solid #8898BF;background: #C8D8FF;font-size:1px"></div>
<div style="padding:5px;font-size:small">
<ul>
<li>Lock <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">m</span> (blocking).</li>
<li>While (<span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">w</span> or <span class="texhtml"><i>r</i> &gt; 0</span>):
<ul>
<li><span class="texhtml">wait <i>c</i>, <i>m</i></span></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Set <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">w</span> to <span class="texhtml">true</span>.</li>
<li>Unlock <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">m</span>.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Each of lock-for-read and lock-for-write has its own inverse operation. Releasing a read lock is done by decrementing <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">r</span> and signalling <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">c</span> if <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">r</span> has become zero (both while holding <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">m</span>), so that one of the threads waiting on <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">c</span> can wake up and lock the R/W lock. Releasing the write lock means setting <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">w</span> to false and broadcasting on <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">c</span> (again while holding <span class="texhtml mvar" style="font-style:italic;">m</span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-herlihy_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-herlihy-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pthreads_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-pthreads-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-butenhof_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-butenhof-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternatives">Alternatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Readers%E2%80%93writer_lock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Alternatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Read-copy-update" title="Read-copy-update">read-copy-update</a> (RCU) algorithm is one solution to the readers–writers problem. RCU is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lock-free_and_wait-free_algorithms" title="Lock-free and wait-free algorithms">wait-free</a> for readers. The <a href="/wiki/Linux_kernel" title="Linux kernel">Linux kernel</a> implements a special solution for few writers called <a href="/wiki/Seqlock" title="Seqlock">seqlock</a>.</p>
