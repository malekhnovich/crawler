<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">James Rumbaugh</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>James E. Rumbaugh</b> (born August 22, 1947) is an American <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Object-oriented" title="Object-oriented">object-oriented</a> methodologist<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InformIT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-InformIT-1">[1]</a></sup> who is best known for his work in creating the <a href="/wiki/Object-modeling_technique" title="Object-modeling technique">Object Modeling Technique</a> (OMT) and the <a href="/wiki/Unified_Modeling_Language" title="Unified Modeling Language">Unified Modeling Language</a> (UML).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Rumbaugh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Bethlehem,_Pennsylvania" title="Bethlehem, Pennsylvania">Bethlehem, Pennsylvania</a>, Rumbaugh received a B.S. in <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a> (MIT), an M.S. in <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a> from the <a href="/wiki/California_Institute_of_Technology" title="California Institute of Technology">California Institute of Technology</a> (Caltech), and received a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> from MIT under Professor <a href="/wiki/Jack_Dennis" title="Jack Dennis">Jack Dennis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InformIT_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-InformIT-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rumbaugh started his career in the 1960s at <a href="/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation" title="Digital Equipment Corporation">Digital Equipment Corporation</a> (DEC) as a lead research scientist. From 1968 to 1994 he worked at the <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> Research and Development Center developing technology, teaching, and consulting. At General Electric he also led the development of <a href="/wiki/Object-modeling_technique" title="Object-modeling technique">Object-modeling technique</a> (OMT), an object modeling language for software modeling and designing.</p>
<p>In 1994, he joined <a href="/wiki/Rational_Software" title="Rational Software">Rational Software</a>, where he worked with <a href="/wiki/Ivar_Jacobson" title="Ivar Jacobson">Ivar Jacobson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grady_Booch" title="Grady Booch">Grady Booch</a> ("the Three Amigos") to develop <a href="/wiki/Unified_Modeling_Language" title="Unified Modeling Language">Unified Modeling Language</a> (UML). Later they merged their software development methologies, OMT, <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_software_engineering" title="Object-oriented software engineering">OOSE</a> and <a href="/wiki/Booch_method" title="Booch method">Booch</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Rational_Unified_Process" title="Rational Unified Process">Rational Unified Process</a> (RUP). In 2003 he moved to <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>, after its acquisition of Rational Software. He retired in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InformIT_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-InformIT-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Rumbaugh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rumbaugh's main research interests are formal description languages, "semantics of computation, tools for programming productivity, and applications using complex algorithms and data structures".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InformIT_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-InformIT-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his graduate work at MIT, Rumbaugh contributed to the development of <a href="/wiki/Dataflow_architecture" title="Dataflow architecture">data flow computer architecture</a>. His thesis described <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parallel_programming_language" title="Parallel programming language">parallel programming language</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parallel_computing" title="Parallel computing">parallel processor computer</a> and a basis for a network architecture, which orients itself at data flow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Rumbaugh made further contributions to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Object_Modeling_Technique" title="Object Modeling Technique">Object Modeling Technique</a>, <a href="/wiki/IDEF4" title="IDEF4">IDEF4</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rational_Unified_Process" title="Rational Unified Process">Rational Unified Process</a> and <a href="/wiki/Unified_Modeling_Language" title="Unified Modeling Language">Unified Modeling Language</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Rumbaugh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rumbaugh has written a number of books about UML and RUP together with <a href="/wiki/Ivar_Jacobson" title="Ivar Jacobson">Ivar Jacobson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grady_Booch" title="Grady Booch">Grady Booch</a>. A selection includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>1975. <i>A Parallel Asynchronous Computer Architecture For Data Flow Programs</i>. MIT thesis</li>
<li>1991. <i>Object-Oriented Modeling and Design</i>. With others. <a href="/wiki/Prentice_Hall" title="Prentice Hall">Prentice Hall</a>, <a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0136298419">ISBN 0-13-629841-9</a>.</li>
<li>1996. <i>OMT insights : perspectives on modeling from the Journal of Object-Oriented Programming</i>. Foreword by James Coplien.</li>
<li>1999. <i>Unified software development process</i></li>
<li>2005. <i>Object-oriented modeling and design with UML</i></li>
</ul>
