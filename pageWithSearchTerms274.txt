<p><b>Systems modeling</b> or <b>system modeling</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Interdisciplinarity" title="Interdisciplinarity">interdisciplinary study</a> of the use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_modeling" title="Scientific modeling">models</a> to conceptualize and construct <a href="/wiki/System" title="System">systems</a> in <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a> and <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT development</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PAW06_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-PAW06-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A common type of systems bull is <a href="/wiki/Function_model" title="Function model">function modeling</a>, with specific techniques such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Functional_Flow_Block_Diagram" title="Functional Flow Block Diagram">Functional Flow Block Diagram</a> and <a href="/wiki/IDEF0" title="IDEF0">IDEF0</a>. These models can be extended using <a href="/wiki/Functional_decomposition" title="Functional decomposition">functional decomposition</a>, and can be linked to <a href="/wiki/Requirement" title="Requirement">requirements</a> models for further systems partition.</p>
<p>Contrasting the functional modeling, another type of systems modeling is <a href="/wiki/Systems_architecture" title="Systems architecture">architectural modeling</a> which uses the <a href="/wiki/Systems_architecture" title="Systems architecture">systems architecture</a> to conceptually model the <a href="/wiki/Structure" title="Structure">structure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Behavior" title="Behavior">behavior</a>, and more <a href="/wiki/View_model" title="View model">views</a> of a system.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Business_Process_Modeling_Notation" title="Business Process Modeling Notation">Business Process Modeling Notation</a> (BPMN), a graphical representation for specifying business processes in a workflow, can also be considered to be a systems modeling language.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Systems_modeling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In business and IT development the term "systems modeling" has multiple meanings. It can relate to:</p>
<ul>
<li>the use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_modeling" title="Scientific modeling">model</a> to conceptualize and construct systems</li>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/Interdisciplinarity" title="Interdisciplinarity">interdisciplinary study</a> of the use of these models</li>
<li>the systems modeling, <a href="/wiki/Systems_analysis" title="Systems analysis">analysis</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Systems_design" title="Systems design">design</a> efforts</li>
<li>the systems modeling and <a href="/wiki/Simulation" title="Simulation">simulation</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/System_dynamics" title="System dynamics">system dynamics</a></li>
<li>any specific systems <a href="/wiki/Modeling_language" title="Modeling language">modeling language</a></li>
</ul>
<p>As a field of study systems modeling has emerged with the development of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/System_theory" title="System theory">system theory</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Systems_sciences" title="Systems sciences">systems sciences</a>.</p>
<p>As a type of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_modeling" title="Scientific modeling">modeling</a> systems modeling is based on <a href="/wiki/Systems_thinking" title="Systems thinking">systems thinking</a> and the systems approach. In business and IT systems modeling contrasts other approaches such as:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agent_based_modeling" title="Agent based modeling">agent based modeling</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Data_modeling" title="Data modeling">data modeling</a> and</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mathematical_modeling" title="Mathematical modeling">mathematical modeling</a></li>
</ul>
<p>In "Methodology for Creating Business Knowledge" (1997) <a href="/wiki/Ingeman_Arbnor" title="Ingeman Arbnor">Arbnor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Bjerke" title="Björn Bjerke">Bjerke</a> the systems approach (systems modeling) was considered to be one of the three basic methodological approaches for gaining business knowledge, beside the analytical approach and the actor's approach (agent based modeling).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Systems_modeling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The function model originates in the 1950s, after in the first half of the 20th century other types of management diagrams had already been developed. The first known <a href="/wiki/Gantt_chart" title="Gantt chart">Gantt chart</a> was developed in 1896 by <a href="/wiki/Karol_Adamiecki" title="Karol Adamiecki">Karol Adamiecki</a>, who called it a <i>harmonogram</i>. Because Adamiecki did not publish his chart until 1931 - and in any case his works were published in either Polish or Russian, languages not popular in <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">the West</a> - the chart now bears the name of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Gantt" title="Henry Gantt">Henry Gantt</a> (1861–1919), who designed his chart around the years 1910-1915 and popularized it in the West.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> One of the first well defined function models, was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Functional_Flow_Block_Diagram" title="Functional Flow Block Diagram">Functional Flow Block Diagram</a> (FFBD) developed by the defense-related <a href="/wiki/TRW_Inc." title="TRW Inc.">TRW</a> Incorporated in the 1950s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In the 1960s it was exploited by the <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> to visualize the time sequence of events in a space systems and flight missions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It is further widely used in classical <a href="/wiki/Systems_engineering" title="Systems engineering">systems engineering</a> to show the order of execution of system functions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TD03_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-TD03-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>One of the earliest pioneering works in <a href="/wiki/Information_system" title="Information system">information systems</a> modeling<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAB07_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-JAB07-8">[8]</a></sup> has been done by Young and Kent (1958), who argued:</p>
<dl>
<dd><i>Since we may be called upon to evaluate different computers or to find alternative ways of organizing current systems it is necessary to have some means of precisely stating a data processing problem independently of mechanization.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p>They aimed for a precise and abstract way of specifying the informational and time characteristics of a <a href="/wiki/Data_processing" title="Data processing">data processing</a> problem, and wanted to create a notation that should enable the <a href="/wiki/Systems_analyst" title="Systems analyst">analyst</a> to organize the problem around any piece of <a href="/wiki/Computer_hardware" title="Computer hardware">hardware</a>. Their efforts was not so much focussed on independent systems analysis, but on creating abstract specification and invariant basis for designing different alternative implementations using different hardware components.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAB07_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-JAB07-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>A next step in IS modeling was taken by <a href="/wiki/CODASYL" title="CODASYL">CODASYL</a>, an IT industry consortium formed in 1959, who essentially aimed at the same thing as Young and Kent: the development of "a proper structure for machine independent problem definition language, at the system level of data processing". This led to the development of a specific IS <a href="/wiki/Information_algebra" title="Information algebra">information algebra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAB07_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-JAB07-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_systems_modeling">Types of systems modeling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Systems_modeling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Types of systems modeling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In business and IT development systems are modeled with different scopes and scales of complexity, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Functional_model" title="Functional model">Functional modeling</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Systems_architecture" title="Systems architecture">Architectural modeling[CPC]</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Business_process_modeling" title="Business process modeling">Business process modeling</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Enterprise_modelling" title="Enterprise modelling">Enterprise modeling</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Further more like <a href="/wiki/Systems_thinking" title="Systems thinking">systems thinking</a>, systems modeling in can be divided into:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Systems_analysis" title="Systems analysis">Systems analysis</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hard_systems" title="Hard systems">Hard systems</a> modeling or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operational_research" title="Operational research">operational research</a> modeling</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soft_system_modeling" title="Soft system modeling">Soft system modeling</a></li>
</ul>
<p>And all other specific types of systems modeling, such as form example <a href="/wiki/Complex_systems" title="Complex systems">complex systems</a> modeling, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dynamical_systems" title="Dynamical systems">dynamical systems</a> modeling, and <a href="/wiki/Critical_systems_thinking" title="Critical systems thinking">critical systems</a> modeling.</p>
