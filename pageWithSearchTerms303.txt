<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">John N. Warfield</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>John Nelson Warfield</b> (November 21, 1925 – November 17, 2009) was an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Systems_scientist" title="Systems scientist">systems scientist</a>, who was professor and director of the Institute for Advanced Study in the Integrative Sciences (IASIS)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> at <a href="/wiki/George_Mason_University" title="George Mason University">George Mason University</a>, and president of the Systems, Man, and Cybernetics Society.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_N._Warfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Warfield was born November 21, 1925 and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, and studied at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Missouri" title="University of Missouri">University of Missouri</a> in Columbia. Originally he majored in <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a> and minored in <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, but his studies were interrupted by <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. After basic training in the U. S. Army Infantry, the Army put him in a specialized training program to study <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a>, which he found very interesting, especially electronics and communications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-background_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-background-2">[2]</a></sup> After the war he completed his original undergraduate program and continued on to get advanced degrees in <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a>. He received the Bachelor of Arts in 1948, Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering in 1948, and Master of Science in Electrical Engineering in 1949 from the University of Missouri, Columbia, Missouri. He received the Doctor of Philosophy degree from <a href="/wiki/Purdue_University" title="Purdue University">Purdue University</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Lafayette,_Indiana" title="West Lafayette, Indiana">West Lafayette, Indiana</a> in 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His major was <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> with a specialty in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communications_engineering" title="Communications engineering">communications engineering</a>.</p>
<p>He gained about 10 years of industrial experience with the firms: Wilcox Electric Company, Battelle Memorial Institute, and Burroughs Corporation. His industrial experience included theoretical and experimental research, electronic development and reliability testing of navigational equipment for jet aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His longest service in this group was with the Battelle Memorial Institute from 1968 to 1974, where he held the title Senior Research Leader.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> At Battelle, and later at Virginia and George Mason universities, he developed the sociotechnology of <a href="/wiki/OPMS" title="OPMS">Interpretive Structural Modeling</a> (ISM) and developed Interactive Management <a class="external autonumber" href="http://sorach.com/items/imdlee.pdf" rel="nofollow">[1]</a> in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Christakis" title="Alexander Christakis">Alexander Christakis</a> from 1979 till 1989.</p>
<p>He was elected President of the <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Systems,_Man,_and_Cybernetics_Society" title="IEEE Systems, Man, and Cybernetics Society">Systems, Man, and Cybernetics Society</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Electrical_and_Electronics_Engineers" title="Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers">Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers</a> IEEE, and of the <a href="/wiki/International_Society_for_the_Systems_Sciences" title="International Society for the Systems Sciences">International Society for the Systems Sciences</a> (formerly called the Society for General Systems Research). He served as Editor of the IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics from 1968 to 1971, and as founding Editor-in-Chief of the Pergamon journal <i>Systems Research</i>, during the period 1981-1990. Warfield was a member of the Academic Committee of the <a href="/wiki/International_Encyclopedia_of_Systems_and_Cybernetics" title="International Encyclopedia of Systems and Cybernetics">International Encyclopedia of Systems and Cybernetics</a>.</p>
<p>He was a Life Fellow of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers, and holds that organization's Centennial Medal. He was a member of the Association for Integrative Studies, and in the Board of Governors of the International Society for Panetics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006 John N. Warfield was awarded the Joseph G. Wohl Award for Career Achievement at the 2006 annual meeting of the IEEE Systems, Man, and Cybernetics Society. This is the highest award given by the society, and is not awarded every year. He was awarded for his contributions to systems engineering concepts, methodology, design, education and management. Warfield was also awarded the IEEE Third Millennium Medal.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_N._Warfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Warfield was the author of more than 10 books and 100 papers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> His books:</p>
<ul>
<li>1958. <i>Synthesis of Linear Communications Networks</i>. with G. E. Knausenberger, New York: McGraw-Hill.</li>
<li>1959. <i>Introduction to Electronic Analog Computers</i>. Englewood Cliffs: Prentice-Hall.</li>
<li>1963. <i>Principles of Logic Design</i>. Boston: Ginn and Company.</li>
<li>1976. <i>Societal Systems: Planning, Policy, and Complexity</i>. New York: Wiley Interscience.</li>
<li>1990. <i>A Science of Generic Design: Managing Complexity through Systems Design</i>. Ames, IA: Iowa State University Press 1994.</li>
<li>1994. <i>A Handbook of Interactive Management</i>. With Roxana Cárdenas, Ames, IA: Iowa State University Press 1994.</li>
<li>2002. <i>Understanding Complexity: Thought and Behavior</i>. AJAR Publishing Company, Palm Harbor, FL.</li>
<li>2003. <i>The Mathematics of Structure</i>. AJAR Publishing Company, Palm Harbor, FL.</li>
<li>2006. <i>An Introduction to Systems Sciences</i>. World Scientific, Singapore</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Articles, papers and monographs, a selection:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>1956. <i>Systems Engineering</i>. United States Department of Commerce PB111801.</li>
<li>1957. "How to Improve Systems Engineering". <i>Aeronautical Engineering Review</i>, 16(7), July, 1957, 50-51.</li>
<li>1969. "What is System Planning?". With R.W. House, in: <i>Automatica</i> Vol. 5, 1969, pp. 151–157.</li>
<li>1972. <i>A Unified Systems Engineering Concept</i>. With J. D. Hill, <i>et al.</i>, <i>Columbus: Battelle Memorial Institute, Monograph No. 1</i>, June, 1972.</li>
<li>1974. <i>Structuring Complex Systems</i>. <i>Columbus: Battelle Memorial Institute Monograph No. 4</i>, April, 1974</li>
<li>1987. "Dimensionality" with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Christakis" title="Alexander Christakis">Alexander Christakis</a> Systems Research 4, pp. 127–137</li>
<li>2003. "A Proposal for Systems Science". In: Systems Research and Behavioral Science, Vol. 20 (2003), pp. 507–520.</li>
<li>2003. "Autobiographical Retrospectives: Discovering Systems Science". In: <i>International Journal of General Systems</i>, December 2003 Vol 32 (6), pp. 525–563.</li>
</ul>
