<p><b>Engineers</b> design materials, structures, and systems while considering the limitations imposed by practicality, regulation, safety, and cost.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bls_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bls-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nspe_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nspe-2">[2]</a></sup> The word <i>engineer (</i>from the Latin <i>ingeniator</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><i>)</i> is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> words <i>ingeniare</i> ("to contrive, devise") and <i>ingenium</i> ("cleverness").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The foundation education of an engineer is typically a 4-year bachelor's degree in an engineering discipline plus 4-6 years peer reviewed professional practice.</p>
<p>The work of engineers forms the link between scientific discoveries and their subsequent applications to human needs and quality of life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bls_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bls-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1960, the Conference of Engineering Societies of Western Europe and the United States of America defined "professional engineer" as follows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>A professional engineer is competent by virtue of his/her fundamental education and training to apply the scientific method and outlook to the analysis and solution of engineering problems. He/she is able to assume personal responsibility for the development and application of engineering science and knowledge, notably in research, design, construction, manufacturing, superintending, managing and in the education of the engineer. His/her work is predominantly intellectual and varied and not of a routine mental or physical character. It requires the exercise of original thought and judgement and the ability to supervise the technical and administrative work of others. His/her education will have been such as to make him/her capable of closely and continuously following progress in his/her branch of engineering science by consulting newly published works on a worldwide basis, assimilating such information and applying it independently. He/she is thus placed in a position to make contributions to the development of engineering science or its applications. His/her education and training will have been such that he/she will have acquired a broad and general appreciation of the engineering sciences as well as thorough insight into the special features of his/her own branch. In due time he/she will be able to give authoritative technical advice and to assume responsibility for the direction of important tasks in his/her branch.</p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Roles_and_expertise">Roles and expertise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Roles and expertise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Engineers develop new technological solutions. During the <a href="/wiki/Engineering_design_process" title="Engineering design process">engineering design process</a>, the responsibilities of the engineer may include defining problems, conducting and narrowing research, analyzing criteria, finding and analyzing solutions, and making decisions. Much of an engineer's time is spent on <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">researching</a>, locating, applying, and transferring information.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Indeed, research suggests engineers spend 56% of their time engaged in various information behaviours, including 14% actively searching for information.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robinson_.282010.29_JASIST_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson_.282010.29_JASIST-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Engineers must weigh different design choices on their merits and choose the solution that best matches the requirements. Their crucial and unique task is to identify, understand, and interpret the constraints on a design in order to produce a successful result.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Analysis">Analysis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Analysis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-23805-1665,_Ingenieure_mit_Konstruktionsplan.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="572" data-file-width="800" height="157" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-23805-1665%2C_Ingenieure_mit_Konstruktionsplan.jpg/220px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-23805-1665%2C_Ingenieure_mit_Konstruktionsplan.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-23805-1665%2C_Ingenieure_mit_Konstruktionsplan.jpg/330px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-23805-1665%2C_Ingenieure_mit_Konstruktionsplan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-23805-1665%2C_Ingenieure_mit_Konstruktionsplan.jpg/440px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-23805-1665%2C_Ingenieure_mit_Konstruktionsplan.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Engineers apply techniques of <a href="/wiki/Engineering_analysis" title="Engineering analysis">engineering analysis</a> in testing, production, or maintenance. Analytical engineers may supervise production in factories and elsewhere, determine the causes of a process failure, and test output to maintain quality. They also estimate the <a href="/wiki/Time_and_motion_study" title="Time and motion study">time and cost required to complete projects</a>. Supervisory engineers are responsible for major components or entire projects. Engineering analysis involves the application of scientific analytic principles and processes to reveal the properties and state of the system, device or mechanism under study. Engineering analysis proceeds by separating the engineering design into the mechanisms of operation or failure, analyzing or estimating each component of the operation or failure mechanism in isolation, and re-combining the components. They may <a href="/wiki/Risk_analysis_(engineering)" title="Risk analysis (engineering)">analyze risk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Many engineers use computers to produce and analyze designs, to simulate and test how a machine, structure, or system operates, to generate specifications for parts, to monitor the quality of products, and to control the efficiency of processes.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Specialization_and_management">Specialization and management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Specialization and management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Firing_Room_-2_During_Apollo_12_CDDT_-_GPN-2000-000632.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2400" data-file-width="3000" height="240" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Firing_Room_-2_During_Apollo_12_CDDT_-_GPN-2000-000632.jpg/300px-Firing_Room_-2_During_Apollo_12_CDDT_-_GPN-2000-000632.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Firing_Room_-2_During_Apollo_12_CDDT_-_GPN-2000-000632.jpg/450px-Firing_Room_-2_During_Apollo_12_CDDT_-_GPN-2000-000632.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Firing_Room_-2_During_Apollo_12_CDDT_-_GPN-2000-000632.jpg/600px-Firing_Room_-2_During_Apollo_12_CDDT_-_GPN-2000-000632.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Firing_Room_-2_During_Apollo_12_CDDT_-_GPN-2000-000632.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
NASA Launch Control Center Firing Room 2 as it appeared in the Apollo era</div>

<p>Most engineers specialize in one or more <a href="/wiki/List_of_engineering_branches" title="List of engineering branches">engineering disciplines</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bls_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-bls-1">[1]</a></sup> Numerous specialties are recognized by professional societies, and each of the major branches of engineering has numerous subdivisions. Civil engineering, for example, includes structural and transportation engineering, and materials engineering includes ceramic, metallurgical, and polymer engineering. Engineers also may specialize in one industry, such as motor vehicles, or in one type of technology, such as turbines or semiconductor materials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bls_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-bls-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Several recent studies have investigated how engineers spend their time; that is, the work tasks they perform and how their time is distributed among these. Research<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robinson_.282010.29_JASIST_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson_.282010.29_JASIST-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robinson_.282012.29_DS_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson_.282012.29_DS-13">[13]</a></sup> suggests that there are several key themes present in engineers’ work: (1) technical work (i.e., the application of science to product development); (2) social work (i.e., interactive communication between people); (3) computer-based work; (4) information behaviours. Amongst other more detailed findings, a recent <a href="/wiki/Work_sampling" title="Work sampling">work sampling</a> study<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robinson_.282012.29_DS_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson_.282012.29_DS-13">[13]</a></sup> found that engineers spend 62.92% of their time engaged in technical work, 40.37% in social work, and 49.66% in computer-based work. Furthermore, there was considerable overlap between these different types of work, with engineers spending 24.96% of their time engaged in technical and social work, 37.97% in technical and non-social, 15.42% in non-technical and social, and 21.66% in non-technical and non-social.</p>
<p>Engineering is also an information intensive field, with research finding that engineers spend 55.8% of their time engaged in various different information behaviours, including 14.2% actively <a href="/wiki/Information_seeking" title="Information seeking">seeking information</a> from other people (7.8%) and information repositories such as documents and databases (6.4%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robinson_.282010.29_JASIST_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson_.282010.29_JASIST-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The time engineers spend engaged in such activities is also reflected in the <a href="/wiki/Competence_(human_resources)" title="Competence (human resources)">competencies</a> required in engineering roles. In addition to engineers’ core technical competence, research has also demonstrated the critical nature of their personal attributes, project management skills, and cognitive abilities to success in the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_engineers">Types of engineers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Types of engineers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_engineering_branches" title="List of engineering branches">List of engineering branches</a></div>
<p>There are many branches of engineering, each of which specializes in specific technologies and products. Typically engineers will have deep knowledge in one area and basic knowledge in related areas. For example, mechanical engineering curricula typically includes introductory courses in electrical engineering and software engineering.</p>
<p>When developing a product, engineers typically work in interdisciplinary teams. For example, when building <a href="/wiki/Robot" title="Robot">robots</a> an engineering team will typically have at least three types of engineers. A mechanical engineer would design the body and actuators. An electrical engineer would design the power systems, sensors, and control circuitry. Finally, a software engineer would develop the software that makes the robot behave properly.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethics">Ethics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Ethics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Engineering_ethics" title="Engineering ethics">Engineering ethics</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Challenger_explosion.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2879" data-file-width="3555" height="178" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Challenger_explosion.jpg/220px-Challenger_explosion.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Challenger_explosion.jpg/330px-Challenger_explosion.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Challenger_explosion.jpg/440px-Challenger_explosion.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Challenger_explosion.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Challenger_disaster" title="Space Shuttle Challenger disaster"><i>Challenger</i> disaster</a> is held as a case study of <a href="/wiki/Engineering_ethics" title="Engineering ethics">engineering ethics</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Engineers have obligations to the public, their clients, employers, and the profession. Many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Engineering_societies" title="Engineering societies">engineering societies</a> have established codes of practice and codes of ethics to guide members and inform the public at large. Each engineering discipline and professional society maintains a code of ethics, which the members pledge to uphold. Depending on their specializations, engineers may also be governed by specific statute, whistleblowing, product liability laws, and often the principles of <a href="/wiki/Business_ethics" title="Business ethics">business ethics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Engg_Ringing_CU.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1608" data-file-width="972" height="281" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e5/Engg_Ringing_CU.jpg/170px-Engg_Ringing_CU.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e5/Engg_Ringing_CU.jpg/255px-Engg_Ringing_CU.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e5/Engg_Ringing_CU.jpg/340px-Engg_Ringing_CU.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Engg_Ringing_CU.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
An engineer receiving his <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Engineer" title="Order of the Engineer">Order of the Engineer</a> ring in a ceremony at <a href="/wiki/Wayne_State_University" title="Wayne State University">Wayne State University</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Some graduates of engineering programs in North America may be recognized by the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Ring" title="Iron Ring">Iron Ring</a> or <a href="/wiki/Engineer%27s_Ring" title="Engineer's Ring">Engineer's Ring</a>, a ring made of iron or stainless steel that is worn on the little finger of the dominant hand. This tradition began in 1925 in Canada with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ritual_of_the_Calling_of_an_Engineer" title="The Ritual of the Calling of an Engineer">The Ritual of the Calling of an Engineer</a>, where the ring serves as a symbol and reminder of the engineer's obligations to the engineering profession. In 1972, the practice was adopted by several colleges in the United States including members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Engineer" title="Order of the Engineer">Order of the Engineer</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Engineering_education" title="Engineering education">Engineering education</a></div>
<p>Most engineering programs involve a concentration of study in an engineering specialty, along with courses in both mathematics and the physical and life sciences. Many programs also include courses in general engineering and applied accounting. A design course, often accompanied by a computer or laboratory class or both, is part of the curriculum of most programs. Often, general courses not directly related to engineering, such as those in the social sciences or humanities, also are required.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Educational_accreditation" title="Educational accreditation">Accreditation</a> is the process by which engineering programs are evaluated by an external body to determine if applicable standards are met. The <a href="/wiki/Washington_Accord" title="Washington Accord">Washington Accord</a> serves as an international accreditation agreement for academic engineering degrees, recognizing the substantial equivalency in the standards set by many major national engineering bodies. In the United States, post-secondary degree programs in engineering are accredited by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Accreditation_Board_for_Engineering_and_Technology" title="Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology">Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulation">Regulation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Regulation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Regulation_and_licensure_in_engineering" title="Regulation and licensure in engineering">Regulation and licensure in engineering</a></div>
<p>In many countries, engineering tasks such as the design of bridges, electric power plants, industrial equipment, machine design and chemical plants, must be approved by a licensed professional engineer. Most commonly titled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professional_Engineer" title="Professional Engineer">Professional Engineer</a> is a license to practice and is indicated with the use of <a href="/wiki/Post-nominal_letters" title="Post-nominal letters">post-nominal letters</a>; PE or P.Eng. These are common in North America, as is European Engineer (<a href="/wiki/European_Engineer" title="European Engineer">EUR ING</a>) in Europe. The practice of engineering in the UK is not a regulated profession but the control of the titles of <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Engineer_(UK)" title="Chartered Engineer (UK)">Chartered Engineer (CEng)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Engineering_technologist#United_Kingdom" title="Engineering technologist">Incorporated Engineer (IEng)</a> is regulated. These titles are protected by law and are subject to strict requirements defined by the Engineering Council UK. The title CEng is in use in much of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a>.</p>
<p>Many skilled / semi-skilled trades and <a href="/wiki/Engineering_technician" title="Engineering technician">engineering technicians</a> in the UK call themselves engineers. A growing movement in the UK is to legally protect the title 'Engineer' so that only professional engineers can use it; a <a href="/wiki/Petition" title="Petition">petition</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> was started to further this cause.</p>
<p>In the United States, licensure is generally attainable through combination of <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, pre-examination (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fundamentals_of_Engineering_exam" title="Fundamentals of Engineering exam">Fundamentals of Engineering exam</a>), examination (Professional Engineering Exam),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and engineering experience (typically in the area of 5+ years). Each state tests and licenses <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professional_Engineer" title="Professional Engineer">Professional Engineers</a>. Currently most states do not license by specific engineering discipline, but rather provide generalized licensure, and trust engineers to use professional judgement regarding their individual competencies; this is the favoured approach of the professional societies. Despite this, however, at least one of the examinations required by most states is actually focused on a particular discipline; candidates for licensure typically choose the category of examination which comes closest to their respective expertise.</p>
<p>In Canada, the profession in each province is governed by its own engineering association. For instance, in the Province of British Columbia an engineering graduate with four or more years of post graduate experience in an engineering-related field and passing exams in ethics and law will need to be registered by the Association for Professional Engineers and Geoscientists (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APEGBC" title="APEGBC">APEGBC</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> in order to become a Professional Engineer and be granted the professional designation of P.Eng allowing one to practice engineering.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Continental_Europe" title="Continental Europe">Continental Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and elsewhere the title is limited by law to people with an engineering degree and the use of the title by others is illegal. In <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, the title is limited to people who both hold an engineering degree and have passed a professional qualification examination (<i>Esame di Stato</i>). In <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, professional engineer titles and accredited engineering degrees are regulated and certified by the <i><a href="/wiki/Ordem_dos_Engenheiros" title="Ordem dos Engenheiros">Ordem dos Engenheiros</a></i>. In the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, the title "engineer" (Ing.) is given to people with a (masters) degree in chemistry, technology or economics for historical and traditional reasons. In <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, the academic title of "Diploma Engineer" is awarded after completion of the five-year engineering study course and the title of "Certified Engineer" is awarded after completion of the four-year course of engineering studies at a Technological Educational Institute (TEI).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_among_countries">Differences among countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Differences among countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Brunel.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3812" data-file-width="3087" height="272" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Brunel.jpg/220px-Brunel.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Brunel.jpg/330px-Brunel.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Brunel.jpg/440px-Brunel.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Brunel.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
19th century engineer <a href="/wiki/Isambard_Kingdom_Brunel" title="Isambard Kingdom Brunel">Isambard Kingdom Brunel</a> by the launching chains of the <a href="/wiki/SS_Great_Eastern" title="SS Great Eastern">SS Great Eastern</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The perception and definition of engineer varies across countries and continents. British school children in the 1950s were brought up with stirring tales of "the Victorian Engineers", chief amongst whom were the <a href="/wiki/Isambard_Kingdom_Brunel" title="Isambard Kingdom Brunel">Brunels</a>, the <a href="/wiki/George_Stephenson" title="George Stephenson">Stephensons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Telford" title="Thomas Telford">Telford</a> and their contemporaries. In the UK, "engineering" was more recently perceived as an industry sector consisting of employers and employees loosely termed "engineers" who included the semi-skilled trades. However, the 21st-century view, especially amongst the more educated members of society, is to reserve the term Engineer to describe a university-educated practitioner of ingenuity represented by the Chartered (or Incorporated) Engineer. However, a large proportion of the UK public still sees Engineers as semi skilled tradespeople with a high school education.</p>
<p>In the US and Canada, engineering is a regulated profession whose practice and practitioners are licensed and governed by law. A 2002 study by the Ontario Society of Professional Engineers revealed that engineers are the third most respected professionals behind doctors and pharmacists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Auto_Rice_Milling_Plants.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="800" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Auto_Rice_Milling_Plants.jpg/250px-Auto_Rice_Milling_Plants.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Auto_Rice_Milling_Plants.jpg/375px-Auto_Rice_Milling_Plants.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Auto_Rice_Milling_Plants.jpg/500px-Auto_Rice_Milling_Plants.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Auto_Rice_Milling_Plants.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Auto rice milling machinery in Bangladesh</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">Indian subcontinent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, Middle East, Africa, and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, engineering is one of the most sought after undergraduate courses, inviting thousands of applicants to show their ability in highly competitive entrance examinations.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Egypt" title="Education in Egypt">educational system</a> makes engineering the second-most-respected profession in the country (after medicine); engineering colleges at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egyptian_universities" title="Egyptian universities">Egyptian universities</a> require extremely high marks on the General Certificate of Secondary Education (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">الثانوية العامة</span>‎‎ <i>al-Thānawiyyah al-`Āmmah</i>)—on the order of 97 or 98%—and are thus considered (along with the colleges of medicine, natural science, and pharmacy) to be among the "pinnacle colleges" (<span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">كليات القمة</span> <i>kullīyāt al-qimmah</i>).</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overseas_Filipino" title="Overseas Filipino">Filipino communities overseas</a>, engineers who are either Filipino or not, especially those who also profess other jobs at the same time, are addressed and introduced as <i>Engineer</i>, rather than <i>Sir/Madam</i> in speech or <i>Mr./Mrs./Ms.</i> (<i>G./Gng./Bb.</i> in Filipino) before surnames. That word is used either in itself or before the given name or surname.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="French_.22Ing.C3.A9nieur.22_title">French "Ingénieur" title</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title='Edit section: French "Ingénieur" title'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It is sometimes told by urban legends that in France, the "Ingénieur" title refers only to membership of the French executive elite and has no relation to technological skills. This is false, engineer is the title of someone who succeeded in engineers schools. There are many different kind of engineer schools in France like in other countries. Some engineer schools are more famous than others. Examples of French famous engineer schools are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polytechnique" title="Polytechnique">Polytechnique</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supelec" title="Supelec">Supelec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Institut_national_des_sciences_appliqu%C3%A9es" title="Institut national des sciences appliquées">Institut national des sciences appliquées</a>, <a href="/wiki/Institut_Mines-T%C3%A9l%C3%A9com" title="Institut Mines-Télécom">Institut Mines-Télécom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arts_et_M%C3%A9tiers_ParisTech" title="Arts et Métiers ParisTech">Ecole nationale supérieure d'arts et métiers</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_Centrale_Paris" title="École Centrale Paris">École Centrale Paris</a>. Polytechnique and ENSAM have their roots in the French revolution and some of their alumni become famous either as scientists (<a href="/wiki/Henri_Poincar%C3%A9" title="Henri Poincaré">Henri Poincaré</a>), CEO of international companies (<a href="/wiki/Bernard_Arnault" title="Bernard Arnault">Bernard Arnault</a>) or as politicians (<a href="/wiki/Val%C3%A9ry_Giscard_d%27Estaing" title="Valéry Giscard d'Estaing">Valéry Giscard d'Estaing</a>).</p>
<p>Polytechnique is even different of other engineer schools as education lasts 6 years instead of 5, with the last year being of specialization in one specific technique. It is also a military school. Most schools of higher education that were created during the French revolution have a special status in French people mind. They helped to make the transition from a mostly agricultural country of late 18th century to the industrial state that France was in the 19th century. A great part of 19th century France's richness was created by engineers coming from Polytechnique or Ecole des mines. This was also the case after the WWII, when France had to be rebuilt.</p>
<p>Before the "réforme <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Haby" title="René Haby">René Haby</a>" in the 70's, it was very difficult to become a French engineer (hence the term "faire les <a href="/wiki/Grandes_%C3%A9coles" title="Grandes écoles">Grandes Écoles</a>" in language of older people), nowadays after the Haby reform and a string of further reforms <a href="/wiki/Modernization_plans_of_French_universities" title="Modernization plans of French universities">Modernization plans of French universities</a> it is much more common to access those schools and the French elite comes more from <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_nationale_d%27administration" title="École nationale d'administration">École nationale d'administration</a> for managers or politicians and <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_normale_sup%C3%A9rieure" title="École normale supérieure">École normale supérieure</a> for scientists. Engineers are less highlighted in current French economy as industry provides less than a quarter of the GDP.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_culture">Corporate culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Corporate culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In companies and other organizations, there is sometimes a tendency to undervalue people with advanced technological and scientific skills compared to celebrities, fashion practitioners, entertainers and <a href="/wiki/Management" title="Management">managers</a>. In his book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mythical_Man-Month" title="The Mythical Man-Month">The Mythical Man-Month</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Fred Brooks Jr says that managers think of senior people as "too valuable" for technical tasks, and that management jobs carry higher prestige. He tells how some laboratories, such as Bell Labs, abolish all job titles to overcome this problem: a professional employee is a "member of the technical staff." IBM maintain a dual ladder of advancement; the corresponding managerial and engineering or scientific rungs are equivalent. Brooks recommends that structures need to be changed; the boss must give a great deal of attention to keeping his managers and his technical people as interchangeable as their talents allow.</p>
